# Unit 31 - have to and must

## Section A - have to (necessity/obligation)

**I have to** do something = it is necessary to do it, I am obliged to do it:
- You can't turn right here. You **have to turn** left.
- I **have to wear** glasses for reading.
- Robert can't come out this evening. He **has to work** late.
- Last week Tina broke her arm and **had to go** to hospital.

### Questions and negatives use do/does/did:
- What **do** I **have to do** to get a new driving licence?
- Karen **doesn't have to work** Saturdays.
- '**Did** you **have to wait** a long time for a bus?' 'No, only ten minutes.'

### Other forms: I'll have to, I'm going to have to, I might have to, I may have to:
- They can't repair my computer, so I'**ll have to buy** a new one.
- We **might have to change** our plans.

## Section B - must vs have to

**Must** is similar to **have to**: I **must go**. *or* I **have to go**.

### Difference in usage:
- **must** or **have to** = your own opinion or recommendation: "You **must phone** her." / "You **have to meet** him."
- **have to** (*not usually* must) = obligation as a fact: "I **have to work** from 8.30 to 5.30 every day."
- **must** in written rules: "Applications **must be received** by 18 May." / "Seat belts **must be worn**."
- **had to** for the past (*not* must): "I **had to** leave early." (*not* I must)

## Section C - mustn't vs don't have to (COMPLETELY DIFFERENT)

- **mustn't** = don't do it (prohibition): "You **mustn't tell** anyone." (= don't tell anyone)
- **don't have to** = not necessary (but you can if you want): "You **don't have to come** with me. I can go alone."

## Section D - have got to

**have got to** = **have to**:
- I'**ve got to** work tomorrow. *or* I **have to** work tomorrow.
- When **has** Helen **got to** go? *or* When **does** Helen **have to** go?
