# Unit 53 - Verb + -ing (enjoy doing / stop doing etc.)

## A: Verbs followed by -ing

After **enjoy**, **mind** and **suggest**, we use **-ing** *(not to ...)*:
- I **enjoy reading**. *(not enjoy to read)*
- Would you **mind closing** the door?
- Chris **suggested going** to the cinema.

### More verbs followed by -ing:
**stop, finish, recommend, consider, admit, deny, avoid, imagine, risk, fancy**

- Suddenly everybody **stopped talking**.
- I'll do the shopping when I've **finished cleaning** the flat.
- He tried to **avoid answering** my question.
- I don't **fancy going** out this evening.
- Have you ever **considered going** to live in another country?
- They said they were innocent. They **denied doing** anything wrong.

**Negative form:** verb + **not** + -ing:
- When I'm on holiday, I enjoy **not having** to get up early.

## B: -ing after phrasal verbs
- **give up** (= stop): I've **given up buying** newspapers.
- **put off** (= delay until later): You shouldn't **put off telling** him what happened.
- **go on** or **carry on** (= continue): She wants to **go on working** / **carry on working**.
- **keep** or **keep on** (= do something continuously or repeatedly): You **keep interrupting** when I'm talking.

## C: verb + somebody + -ing
- You can't **stop people doing** what they want.
- I can't **imagine George riding** a motorbike.
- I don't **remember her saying** that.
- Sorry to **keep you waiting** so long.

## D: having done/stolen/said etc.

When you talk about finished actions, you can say **having done/stolen/said** etc.:
- They admitted **having stolen** the money.

But it is not necessary to use **having (done)**. You can say:
- They admitted **stealing** the money.
- I now regret **saying** that. *or* I now regret **having said** that.

## E: Other structures with admit, deny, suggest, recommend

You can also say:
- They **denied** (that) **they had done** anything wrong. (= They **denied doing** ...)
- Chris **suggested** (that) **we go** to the cinema. (= Chris **suggested going** ...)
- I **recommend** (that) **you travel** by train. (= I **recommend travelling** ...)
