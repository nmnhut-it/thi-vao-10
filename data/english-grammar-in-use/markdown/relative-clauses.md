# Relative Clauses - Menh de quan he

## Gioi thieu (Introduction)

A relative clause (menh de quan he) is a part of a sentence that gives extra information about a noun. It begins with a relative pronoun such as **who, which, that, whose, whom, where,** or **when**.

> The woman **who lives next door to me** is a doctor.

The relative clause "who lives next door to me" tells us which woman we are talking about.

---

## 1. Dai tu quan he (Relative Pronouns)

| Dai tu | Dung cho | Vai tro | Vi du |
|---|---|---|---|
| **who** | nguoi (people) | chu ngu / tan ngu | The man **who** called you is my brother. |
| **which** | vat / dong vat (things) | chu ngu / tan ngu | The book **which** I bought is interesting. |
| **that** | nguoi hoac vat (both) | chu ngu / tan ngu | The girl **that** won the prize is my friend. |
| **whose** | so huu (possession) | thay cho his/her/its/their | The boy **whose** mother is a teacher studies hard. |
| **whom** | nguoi (people) | tan ngu (formal) | The woman **whom** I met was very kind. |
| **where** | noi chon (place) | trang ngu chi noi chon | The hotel **where** we stayed was excellent. |
| **when** | thoi gian (time) | trang ngu chi thoi gian | I remember the day **when** we first met. |

### Chi tiet tung dai tu:

**WHO** - dung cho nguoi, lam chu ngu hoac tan ngu:
- The woman **who** lives next door is a doctor. (who = subject)
- The woman **who** I wanted to see was away. (who = object)

**WHICH** - dung cho vat, lam chu ngu hoac tan ngu:
- I do not like stories **which** have unhappy endings. (which = subject)
- The dress **which** Lisa bought does not fit her. (which = object)

**THAT** - co the thay the cho who hoac which:
- The woman **that** lives next door is a doctor. (= who)
- The dress **that** Lisa bought does not fit her. (= which)

**WHOSE** - chi su so huu, thay cho his/her/its/their:
- I met a man **whose** sister works at the hospital. (= his sister)
- We saw a house **whose** roof was damaged. (= its roof)

**WHOM** - dung cho nguoi, lam tan ngu (formal hon who):
- The man **whom** I spoke to was very helpful.
- The people **whom** we invited did not come.

**WHERE** - thay the cho "in/at which" chi noi chon:
- The restaurant **where** we had lunch was very good. (= at which)
- I want to live in a city **where** there are lots of parks.

**WHEN** - thay the cho "in/on/at which" chi thoi gian:
- I will never forget the day **when** we first met. (= on which)
- Do you remember the year **when** we moved here?

## 2. Menh de quan he xac dinh va khong xac dinh (Defining vs Non-defining)

### a) Menh de quan he xac dinh (Defining relative clauses)

These clauses are **essential** to the meaning. Without them, we do not know which person or thing is meant. **No commas** are used.

> The woman **who lives next door** is a doctor.
> (Which woman? The one who lives next door.)

> I liked the book **that you recommended**.
> (Which book? The one you recommended.)

### b) Menh de quan he khong xac dinh (Non-defining relative clauses)

These clauses give **extra information** that is NOT essential. The person or thing is already identified. **Commas** are required.

> My brother Tom, **who lives in London**, is a lawyer.
> (We already know which brother - Tom. The clause adds extra info.)

> The Mekong River, **which flows through Vietnam**, is very long.
> (There is only one Mekong River. The clause is extra information.)

### So sanh:

| Defining (xac dinh) | Non-defining (khong xac dinh) |
|---|---|
| Khong co dau phay | Co dau phay |
| Thong tin can thiet | Thong tin bo sung |
| Co the dung **that** | KHONG dung **that** |
| Co the luoc dai tu (khi la tan ngu) | KHONG luoc dai tu |

## 3. Khi nao dung va KHONG dung "that" (When "that" can and cannot be used)

### Co the dung "that":
- Trong menh de xac dinh, thay cho who hoac which:
  - The man **that** called is my uncle. (= who)
  - The car **that** I bought is blue. (= which)

### KHONG dung "that" khi:
1. **Menh de khong xac dinh** (non-defining - co dau phay):
   - My mother, **who** is 60, still works. (NOT: that)
   - Paris, **which** is the capital of France, is beautiful. (NOT: that)

2. **Sau gioi tu** (after prepositions):
   - The chair **on which** I was sitting broke. (NOT: on that)
   - The man **to whom** I spoke was helpful. (NOT: to that)

### Truong hop nen dung "that" (thay vi which/who):
- Sau **superlatives**: It is the best film **that** I have ever seen.
- Sau **all, everything, nothing, anything, something**: Everything **that** he said was true.
- Sau **the first, the last, the only**: She was the only person **that** understood me.

---

## 4. Luoc dai tu quan he (Omitting the Relative Pronoun)

### Co the luoc khi dai tu la TAN NGU (object) trong menh de xac dinh:

> The woman **(who/that)** I wanted to see was away.
> -> The woman I wanted to see was away. (OK - luoc duoc)

> Did you find the keys **(that/which)** you lost?
> -> Did you find the keys you lost? (OK)

### KHONG luoc khi dai tu la CHU NGU (subject):

> The woman **who** lives next door is a doctor.
> (KHONG the noi: The woman lives next door is a doctor.)

### KHONG luoc trong menh de khong xac dinh:

> My brother, **who** lives in London, is a lawyer.
> (KHONG the luoc "who")

### Cach nhan biet:

Neu sau dai tu quan he la **dong tu** -> dai tu la chu ngu -> KHONG luoc.
Neu sau dai tu quan he la **chu ngu khac** (I, he, she, they...) -> dai tu la tan ngu -> CO THE luoc.

---

## 5. Gioi tu trong menh de quan he (Prepositions in Relative Clauses)

### Vi tri gioi tu: cuoi menh de (informal) hoac truoc dai tu (formal)

> Informal: The man **who** I was talking **to** was very nice.
> Formal: The man **to whom** I was talking was very nice.

> Informal: The chair **which** I was sitting **on** broke.
> Formal: The chair **on which** I was sitting broke.

### Luu y quan trong:
- Khi gioi tu dung truoc, phai dung **whom** (khong dung who) va **which** (khong dung that):
  - The man **to whom** I spoke... (NOT: to who hoac to that)
  - The house **in which** I grew up... (NOT: in that)
- Khi gioi tu dung cuoi, co the dung who/that/which hoac luoc:
  - The man (who/that) I was talking **to**...
  - The chair (which/that) I was sitting **on**...

## 6. Menh de quan he rut gon (Reduced Relative Clauses)

### a) Dung V-ing (present participle) thay cho menh de chu dong:

> The woman **who is talking** to Tom is my sister.
> -> The woman **talking** to Tom is my sister.

> Students **who study** hard will pass the exam.
> -> Students **studying** hard will pass the exam.

### b) Dung V3/ed (past participle) thay cho menh de bi dong:

> The boy **who was injured** in the accident is now in hospital.
> -> The boy **injured** in the accident is now in hospital.

> The money **which was stolen** has not been found.
> -> The money **stolen** has not been found.

### c) Dung to-infinitive:

> He was the first person **who arrived**. -> He was the first person **to arrive**.
> She is the only student **who passed** the exam. -> She is the only student **to pass** the exam.

## 7. Mau chuyen doi thuong gap trong de thi vao 10 (Common Exam Patterns)

### Pattern 1: Noi hai cau thanh mot cau dung menh de quan he
```
The man is my teacher. He helped me yesterday.
-> The man who helped me yesterday is my teacher.

I bought a book. It is very interesting.
-> The book which/that I bought is very interesting.
```

### Pattern 2: Dung whose
```
The girl is very smart. Her father is a professor.
-> The girl whose father is a professor is very smart.
```

### Pattern 3: Dung where / when
```
That is the restaurant. We had dinner there.
-> That is the restaurant where we had dinner.

I remember the day. We first met on that day.
-> I remember the day when we first met.
```

### Pattern 4: Xac dinh vs Khong xac dinh
```
Ha Noi is the capital of Vietnam. It has a population of 8 million.
-> Ha Noi, which is the capital of Vietnam, has a population of 8 million.
(Non-defining: Ha Noi is already identified)
```

### Pattern 5: Rut gon menh de quan he
```
The students who are sitting in the front row are my classmates.
-> The students sitting in the front row are my classmates.

The letter which was written in French was from my pen pal.
-> The letter written in French was from my pen pal.
```

### Pattern 6: Gioi tu + whom/which
```
The woman was very kind. I spoke to her.
-> The woman to whom I spoke was very kind.
-> The woman (who/that) I spoke to was very kind.
```

## 8. Loi thuong gap (Common Mistakes)

| Sai | Dung | Giai thich |
|---|---|---|
| The man which called... | The man **who/that** called... | Dung who/that cho nguoi |
| My sister, that lives in HCM,... | My sister, **who** lives in HCM,... | Khong dung that trong non-defining |
| The man to that I spoke... | The man **to whom** I spoke... | Khong dung that sau gioi tu |
| The woman who I met her... | The woman who I met... | Khong lap lai dai tu (her) |
| The house where I live in it... | The house where I live... | where = in which, khong them in it |
| The man lives next door is nice. | The man **who** lives next door... | Khong luoc chu ngu |

## 9. Bai tap nhanh (Quick Practice)

**Noi hai cau dung menh de quan he:**

1. The girl is my classmate. She won the singing contest.
   -> The girl **who won the singing contest** is my classmate.

2. I visited the museum. It was built 100 years ago.
   -> The museum **which/that I visited** was built 100 years ago.

3. The woman is a famous writer. Her books are best-sellers.
   -> The woman **whose books are best-sellers** is a famous writer.

4. This is the park. We often play soccer here.
   -> This is the park **where we often play soccer**.

5. July is the month. Most students have their summer holiday then.
   -> July is the month **when most students have their summer holiday**.

6. Da Nang is a beautiful city. It is located in central Vietnam.
   -> Da Nang, **which is located in central Vietnam**, is a beautiful city.
