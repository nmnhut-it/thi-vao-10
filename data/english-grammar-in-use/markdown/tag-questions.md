# Tag Questions (Cau hoi duoi)

## Cau hoi duoi: do you?, isn't it?, won't she?, v.v.

---

## 1. What Are Tag Questions? (Cau hoi duoi la gi?)

A **tag question** (cau hoi duoi) is a short question added to the end of a sentence. We use tag questions to confirm information or to ask someone to agree with us.

- It's a nice day, **isn't it?**
- You speak English, **don't you?**
- She hasn't arrived yet, **has she?**

In Vietnamese, tag questions work like adding "phai khong?", "dung khong?", or "nhi?" at the end of a sentence.

---

## 2. Basic Formation Rules (Quy tac co ban)

### Rule 1: Positive sentence -> Negative tag (Cau khang dinh -> Tag phu dinh)

| Sentence | Tag | Full example |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| She **is** a student, | **isn't she?** | She is a student, isn't she? |
| They **have** finished, | **haven't they?** | They have finished, haven't they? |
| He **can** swim, | **can't he?** | He can swim, can't he? |
| You **will** come, | **won't you?** | You will come, won't you? |
| She **likes** coffee, | **doesn't she?** | She likes coffee, doesn't she? |
| They **went** home, | **didn't they?** | They went home, didn't they? |

### Rule 2: Negative sentence -> Positive tag (Cau phu dinh -> Tag khang dinh)

| Sentence | Tag | Full example |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| She **isn't** happy, | **is she?** | She isn't happy, is she? |
| They **don't** like fish, | **do they?** | They don't like fish, do they? |
| He **won't** be late, | **will he?** | He won't be late, will he? |
| You **haven't** eaten yet, | **have you?** | You haven't eaten yet, have you? |
| She **can't** drive, | **can she?** | She can't drive, can she? |

### How to form the tag (Cach tao tag)

1. **Find the auxiliary verb** (tro dong tu) in the main sentence: am, is, are, was, were, have, has, had, will, would, can, could, shall, should, may, might, must.
2. **If the sentence has no auxiliary**, use **do/does** (present) or **did** (past).
3. **Reverse the polarity**: positive becomes negative, negative becomes positive.
4. **Use the same subject** as a pronoun.

Examples with **do/does/did**:
- You **speak** French, **don't you?** *(present simple -> do)*
- She **plays** tennis, **doesn't she?** *(he/she/it -> does)*
- They **arrived** late, **didn't they?** *(past simple -> did)*

## 3. Special Cases (Cac truong hop dac biet)

These special cases appear frequently in exams. Learn them carefully.

### 3.1 "I am" -> "aren't I?"

When the subject is **I** and the verb is **am**, the tag is **aren't I?** (not "amn't I?"):

- I'm right, **aren't I?**
- I'm late, **aren't I?**
- I'm your friend, **aren't I?**

**Note:** For **I am not**, the tag is **am I?**
- I'm not wrong, **am I?**

### 3.2 "Let's ..." -> "shall we?"

When the sentence begins with **Let's** (= Let us), the tag is always **shall we?**:

- **Let's** go for a walk, **shall we?**
- **Let's** have lunch, **shall we?**
- **Let's** not argue, **shall we?**

### 3.3 Imperatives -> "will you?" / "won't you?"

For imperative sentences (cau menh lenh), the tag is usually **will you?** or **won't you?**:

- **Open** the door, **will you?**
- **Don't** be late, **will you?**
- **Give** me a hand, **won't you?**
- **Be** quiet, **will you?**
- **Don't** forget, **will you?**

### 3.4 Nobody / Nothing / No one -> Positive tag

Words like **nobody, nothing, no one, never, hardly, seldom, rarely, barely** make a sentence negative. Therefore, the tag must be **positive**.

| Sentence | Tag | Note |
|----------|-----|------|
| **Nobody** came, | **did they?** | nobody = no person -> they |
| **Nothing** happened, | **did it?** | nothing -> it |
| **No one** likes him, | **do they?** | no one -> they |
| He **never** goes out, | **does he?** | never = negative |
| She **hardly** eats anything, | **does she?** | hardly = almost not |
| They **seldom** travel, | **do they?** | seldom = rarely |

**Important (Luu y):** Use **they** to refer back to **nobody, no one, somebody, someone, everybody, everyone**.

### 3.5 Somebody / Everyone / Everybody -> "they"

When the subject is an indefinite pronoun referring to people, use **they** in the tag:

- **Somebody** called, **didn't they?**
- **Everyone** is here, **aren't they?**
- **Everybody** enjoyed the party, **didn't they?**
- **Someone** left their bag, **didn't they?**

### 3.6 "There is / There are" -> "isn't there? / aren't there?"

When **there** is the subject, keep **there** in the tag:

- **There** is a problem, **isn't there?**
- **There** are many students, **aren't there?**
- **There** was a lot of traffic, **wasn't there?**
- **There** won't be any trouble, **will there?**

### 3.7 "This / That" -> "isn't it?"

**This** and **that** become **it** in the tag:

- **This** is your book, **isn't it?**
- **That** was a great movie, **wasn't it?**

### 3.8 "I have" vs "I have got"

| Sentence | Tag |
|----------|-----|
| She **has got** a car, | **hasn't she?** |
| She **has** a car, | **doesn't she?** (British) or **hasn't she?** |
| He **had** lunch, | **didn't he?** (action) |

### 3.9 "Used to" -> "didn't + subject?"

- She **used to** live here, **didn't she?**
- He **used to** smoke, **didn't he?**

### 3.10 "Must" cases

| Sentence | Tag |
|----------|-----|
| You **must** go now, | **mustn't you?** |
| He **must** be tired, | **isn't he?** (must = deduction about present) |
| She **must have** arrived, | **hasn't she?** (must = deduction about past) |

## 4. "I think / I believe / I suppose" Sentences

This is a very important rule for the exam (Quy tac rat quan trong cho bai thi).

When the **main clause** has **I think / I believe / I suppose / I expect / I guess / I imagine** + a **subordinate clause**, the tag follows the **subordinate clause**, not the main clause.

### The rule applies when the subject of the main clause is "I"

| Sentence | Tag | Explanation |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| **I think** he is honest, | **isn't he?** | Tag follows "he is honest" |
| **I believe** she will come, | **won't she?** | Tag follows "she will come" |
| **I suppose** you are tired, | **aren't you?** | Tag follows "you are tired" |
| **I expect** they have finished, | **haven't they?** | Tag follows "they have finished" |
| **I guess** it will rain, | **won't it?** | Tag follows "it will rain" |

### Negative transfer (Chuyen phu dinh)

When **I think/believe/suppose** is negative, the **meaning is negative** for the subordinate clause, so the tag is **positive**:

| Sentence | Tag | Explanation |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| **I don't think** he is honest, | **is he?** | "I don't think" = I think he is NOT honest |
| **I don't believe** she can come, | **can she?** | Negative transferred to subordinate clause |
| **I don't suppose** you want to go, | **do you?** | Same pattern |

### When the subject is NOT "I"

When the subject is **he, she, they**, etc., the tag follows the **main clause**:

| Sentence | Tag |
|----------|-----|
| **He thinks** I am wrong, | **doesn't he?** |
| **She believes** it will rain, | **doesn't she?** |
| **They suppose** we are coming, | **don't they?** |

## 5. Intonation (Ngu dieu)

The meaning of a tag question depends on your voice:

### Rising intonation (Ngu dieu len) = Real question
You are genuinely asking and don't know the answer:
- You haven't seen Kate today, **have you?** (voice goes UP = I really want to know)

### Falling intonation (Ngu dieu xuong) = Expecting agreement
You expect the listener to agree with you:
- It's a beautiful day, **isn't it?** (voice goes DOWN = I expect you to agree)

## 6. Answering Tag Questions (Tra loi cau hoi duoi)

This confuses many Vietnamese students. The answer depends on the **fact**, not on the form of the question.

### For negative tags (expecting "yes"):
- "You're coming to the party, aren't you?" -> **"Yes, I am."** (if true)

### For negative sentences with positive tags:
- "You're **not** going out, **are you?**"
  - **"Yes."** = Yes, I AM going out. (Co, toi se di.)
  - **"No."** = No, I am NOT going out. (Khong, toi khong di.)

**Key point (Diem mau chot):** "Yes" always means the positive fact is true. "No" always means the negative fact is true. This is different from Vietnamese, where "Khong" can sometimes confirm a negative question.

| Negative + aux | She isn't happy, | is she? |

| Past simple | They went home, | didn't they? |

## 8. Common Exam Mistakes (Loi thuong gap trong bai thi)

1. **Wrong:** I am late, *amn't I*? **Right:** I am late, **aren't I?**
2. **Wrong:** Nobody came, *didn't he*? **Right:** Nobody came, **did they?**
3. **Wrong:** Let's eat, *will we*? **Right:** Let's eat, **shall we?**
4. **Wrong:** I think she is kind, *don't I*? **Right:** I think she is kind, **isn't she?**
5. **Wrong:** I don't think he is honest, *isn't he*? **Right:** I don't think he is honest, **is he?**
6. **Wrong:** Nothing happened, *didn't they*? **Right:** Nothing happened, **did it?**
7. **Wrong:** He never goes out, *doesn't he*? **Right:** He never goes out, **does he?**
8. **Wrong:** She hardly eats, *does she*? **Right!** (This one IS correct -- hardly is negative.)
