# Unit 37 - can/could/would you ...? etc. (Requests, offers, permission and invitations)

## Section A - Asking people to do things (requests)

Use **can** or **could** to ask people to do things:
- **Can you** wait a moment, please? *or* **Could you** wait a moment, please?
- Helen, **can you** do me a favour?
- Excuse me, **could you** tell me how to get to the bus station?

More polite: **Do you think you could ...?**
- **Do you think you could** take me to the airport?

## Section B - Asking for things

Use **Can (I) have ...?** / **Could (I) have ...?** / **Can (I) get ...?**:
- **Can** I **have** these postcards, please?
- **Could** we **have** the menu, please?
- **May** I **have** these postcards, please? (also possible)

## Section C - Asking to do things (permission)

Use **can I** or **could I** to ask to do something:
- Hello, **can I** speak to Steve, please?
- '**Could I** use your phone charger?' 'Sure.'
- **Do you think I could** borrow your bike?

**May** is also possible (more formal than can or could):
- **May** I **ask you a question**?

Alternative forms: **Do you mind if I ...?** / **Is it all right if I ...?**

## Section D - Offering and inviting

**Can I ...?** to offer:
- '**Can I** help you?' 'No, it's OK. I can manage, thanks.'
- '**Can I** get you some coffee?' 'That would be nice.'

**Would you like ...?** to offer or invite (*not* Do you like):
- '**Would you like** some coffee?' 'No, thanks.'
- '**Would you like** to eat with us tonight?' 'That would be great.'

**I'd like** (= I would like) for polite requests:
- **I'd like** some information about hotels, please.
- **I'd like** to try on this jacket, please.
