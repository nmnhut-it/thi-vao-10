# Passive Voice - Cau bi dong

## Khi nao dung cau bi dong? (When do we use the passive?)

We use the passive when:
- We do not know who did the action (khong biet ai lam): *My bike **was stolen** last night.*
- The doer is not important (nguoi lam khong quan trong): *This bridge **was built** in 1990.*
- The action or result matters more than the doer: *The exam results **will be announced** tomorrow.*

In Vietnamese: Cau bi dong nhan manh vao doi tuong chiu tac dong, khong phai nguoi thuc hien hanh dong.

---

## 1. Cong thuc tong quat (General Formula)

**Active:** Subject + verb + object
**Passive:** Object (becomes subject) + **be** (chia thi) + **past participle** (+ by + agent)

> Active: *The teacher **explains** the lesson.*
> Passive: *The lesson **is explained** (by the teacher).*

---

## 2. Bang cau bi dong theo cac thi (Passive Formation by Tense)

| Thi (Tense) | Active | Passive (be + V3/ed) |
|---|---|---|
| Present simple | He **cleans** the room. | The room **is cleaned**. |
| Present continuous | He **is cleaning** the room. | The room **is being cleaned**. |
| Past simple | He **cleaned** the room. | The room **was cleaned**. |
| Past continuous | He **was cleaning** the room. | The room **was being cleaned**. |
| Present perfect | He **has cleaned** the room. | The room **has been cleaned**. |
| Past perfect | He **had cleaned** the room. | The room **had been cleaned**. |
| Future simple (will) | He **will clean** the room. | The room **will be cleaned**. |
| Future simple (going to) | He **is going to clean** the room. | The room **is going to be cleaned**. |
| Modal verbs (can, must, should, may, might, have to) | He **can clean** the room. | The room **can be cleaned**. |
| Perfect infinitive | He **should have cleaned** the room. | The room **should have been cleaned**. |

**Luu y quan trong (Important notes):**
- Continuous passive dung **being**: *is/was being + V3*
- Perfect passive dung **been**: *has/had been + V3*
- Modal passive: *modal + be + V3*
- Modal perfect passive: *modal + have been + V3*

---

## 3. Cau bi dong voi dong tu co hai tan ngu (Passive with Two Objects)

Some verbs can have two objects (hai tan ngu): **give, send, show, tell, offer, pay, ask, teach, lend**.

> Active: *My grandfather **gave** **me** **this watch**.*

Two possible passive sentences:
> **I was given** this watch (by my grandfather). *(more common - bat dau voi nguoi)*
> **This watch was given** to me (by my grandfather).

More examples:
- *They **offered** Sarah the job.* -> **Sarah was offered** the job. / **The job was offered** to Sarah.
- *Someone **will show** you the way.* -> **You will be shown** the way.
- *They **haven't told** me anything.* -> **I haven't been told** anything.

**Meo thi (Exam tip):** In Vietnamese Grade 9 exams, the version starting with the person (indirect object) is preferred and more commonly tested.

## 4. Cau bi dong voi make, let, see, hear, watch (Perception & Causative Verbs)

### Quy tac quan trong: Them "to" khi chuyen sang bi dong

With **make, see, hear, watch** in active voice, we use bare infinitive (dong tu nguyen the khong "to"). But in passive, we MUST add **to**.

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| They **made** him **work** hard. | He **was made to work** hard. |
| I **saw** her **cross** the street. | She **was seen to cross** the street. |
| We **heard** him **sing** a song. | He **was heard to sing** a song. |
| They **watched** the children **play**. | The children **were watched to play**. |

**Ngoai le voi LET:** "Let" is NOT normally used in the passive. We use **be allowed to** instead.

> Active: *They **let** us go home early.*
> Passive: *We **were allowed to** go home early.* (NOT ~~We were let to go home early.~~)

### Mau cau chuyen doi (Transformation pattern):
```
Active:  S + make/see/hear + O + V(bare)
Passive: O + be + made/seen/heard + TO + V
```

## 5. Cau tuong thuat bi dong (Passive Reporting Structures)

We use these structures with verbs like: **say, believe, think, know, report, expect, consider, allege, understand, suppose**.

### Cau truc 1: It + is/was + V3 + that + clause

> *People **say** that he is rich.*
> -> **It is said that** he is rich.

> *They **believed** that she had left the country.*
> -> **It was believed that** she had left the country.

### Cau truc 2: S + is/was + V3 + to V / to have V3

> *People **say** that he is rich.*
> -> **He is said to be** rich.

> *They **believe** that she left the country.*
> -> **She is believed to have left** the country. *(dung "to have V3" khi hanh dong xay ra truoc)*

### Bang chuyen doi chi tiet:

| Active | Passive (It...) | Passive (S + be + V3 + to...) |
|---|---|---|
| People say he **is** 108. | It **is said** that he is 108. | He **is said to be** 108. |
| They report she **works** hard. | It **is reported** that she works hard. | She **is reported to work** hard. |
| People think he **stole** a car. | It **is thought** that he stole a car. | He **is thought to have stolen** a car. |
| They expected the strike **would** end. | It **was expected** that the strike would end. | The strike **was expected to end** soon. |

### Quy tac chon "to V" hay "to have V3":
- **To V** (to-infinitive): khi hanh dong trong menh de "that" xay ra **cung thoi** voi dong tu chinh.
  - *People say he **is** rich.* -> He is said **to be** rich.
- **To have V3** (perfect infinitive): khi hanh dong trong menh de "that" xay ra **truoc** dong tu chinh.
  - *People say he **was** rich.* -> He is said **to have been** rich.

### "Supposed to" - nghia dac biet:

Ngoai nghia tuong thuat, **be supposed to** con co nghia la "duoc cho la nen" hoac "duoc mong doi":
- *You **are supposed to** wear a uniform.* (= Ban duoc yeu cau mac dong phuc.)
- *She **was supposed to** call me.* (= Co ay le ra phai goi cho toi.)
- *You **are not supposed to** park here.* (= Ban khong duoc phep do xe o day.)

## 6. Cau truc nho lam (Causative: have/get something done)

### Have something done (nho/thue nguoi khac lam)

**Cong thuc:** S + **have** + object + **past participle**

This means you arrange for someone else to do something for you. You do NOT do it yourself.

> *Lisa **had the roof repaired**.* (= Lisa thue nguoi sua mai nha. Co ay khong tu sua.)

Compare:
- *Lisa **repaired** the roof.* (= Co ay tu tay sua.)
- *Lisa **had** the roof **repaired**.* (= Co ay thue nguoi sua.)

### Thu tu tu trong cau:

| Subject | have | Object | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lisa | **had** | the roof | **repaired**. |
| I | **have** | my hair | **cut** every month. |
| We | **are having** | the house | **painted** this week. |
| You | **should have** | your eyes | **tested**. |

### Get something done (tuong tu have something done)

You can use **get** instead of **have** (informal):
- *When are you going to **get the roof repaired**?*
- *I need to **get my phone fixed**.*

### Have/get something done - nghia thu hai (dieu khong mong muon):

We also use this structure when something bad happens to someone:
- *Paul and Karen **had their bags stolen** while travelling.* (= Tui cua ho bi an cap.)
- *Gary **had his nose broken** in a fight.* (= Anh ay bi gay mui.)

### Mau cau chuyen doi cho bai thi:

```
Active:  Someone repairs my car every month.
Passive: My car is repaired every month.
Causative: I have my car repaired every month.
```

## 7. Cac mau chuyen doi thuong gap trong de thi vao 10 (Exam Transformation Patterns)

### Pattern 1: Active -> Passive (co ban)
```
Active:  They built this school in 2005.
Passive: This school was built in 2005.
```

### Pattern 2: Passive voi hai tan ngu
```
Active:  Someone gave me a present.
Passive: I was given a present. / A present was given to me.
```

### Pattern 3: Passive voi make/see/hear
```
Active:  The teacher made the students do homework.
Passive: The students were made to do homework (by the teacher).
```

### Pattern 4: Passive tuong thuat
```
Active:  People say that he is a good doctor.
Passive: It is said that he is a good doctor.
     OR: He is said to be a good doctor.
```

### Pattern 5: Causative
```
Normal:   Someone cut my hair yesterday.
Causative: I had my hair cut yesterday.
```

### Pattern 6: Passive voi modal verbs
```
Active:  You must finish this work today.
Passive: This work must be finished today.
```

### Pattern 7: Passive voi "It is time / It is necessary"
```
It is necessary to finish this report.
-> This report needs to be finished. / This report needs finishing.
```

## 8. Loi thuong gap (Common Mistakes)

| Sai | Dung | Giai thich |
|---|---|---|
| ~~The room was cleaning.~~ | The room **was being cleaned**. | Past continuous passive can "being" |
| ~~He was made work hard.~~ | He **was made to work** hard. | Passive cua "make" phai them "to" |
| ~~It is said he to be rich.~~ | He **is said to be** rich. | Chu ngu di truoc "is said" |
| ~~I had repaired my car.~~ | I **had my car repaired**. | Causative: have + O + V3 |
| ~~She was let go.~~ | She **was allowed to** go. | Khong dung "let" o bi dong |
| ~~The book has written by Tom.~~ | The book **has been written** by Tom. | Perfect passive can "been" |

## 9. Bai tap nhanh (Quick Practice)

**Chuyen sang cau bi dong:**

1. Someone cleans the office every day.
   -> The office **is cleaned** every day.

2. They are building a new hospital.
   -> A new hospital **is being built**.

3. The police have arrested two men.
   -> Two men **have been arrested** (by the police).

4. The teacher made us rewrite the essay.
   -> We **were made to rewrite** the essay (by the teacher).

5. People believe that she is the best student.
   -> It **is believed that** she is the best student.
   -> She **is believed to be** the best student.

6. I didn't cut my hair myself.
   -> I **had my hair cut**. (causative)
