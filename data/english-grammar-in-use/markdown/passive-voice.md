# Passive Voice (Cau truc bi dong)

## 1. Formation: How to Build the Passive

The passive is formed with **be + past participle (V3/Ved)**.

The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence. The doer (nguoi thuc hien) is introduced by **by**, but is often omitted when unknown or unimportant.

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present simple | They **clean** the room every day. | The room **is cleaned** every day. |
| Past simple | Someone **stole** my bike. | My bike **was stolen**. |
| Present continuous | They **are building** a bridge. | A bridge **is being built**. |
| Past continuous | They **were following** us. | We **were being followed**. |
| Present perfect | Someone **has broken** the window. | The window **has been broken**. |
| Past perfect | They **had cooked** the food too long. | The food **had been cooked** too long. |
| Future (will) | They **will finish** the work soon. | The work **will be finished** soon. |
| Future (going to) | They **are going to build** a school. | A school **is going to be built**. |
| Modal verbs | You **must do** it now. | It **must be done** now. |

### Key Pattern

**am/is/are/was/were/been/being + past participle**

### Notes for Exams (Luu y khi thi)

- Only transitive verbs (dong tu co tan ngu) can be made passive.
- Intransitive verbs like *happen, sleep, arrive, die* cannot be passive.
- We say "I **was born** in 2010" (not "I am born") -- always past tense for birth.

## 2. Passive with Modals and Perfect Infinitive

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| modal + **be done** | This letter **should be sent** today. |
| modal + **have been done** | The report **should have been finished** yesterday. |

Common modals in passive: *can, could, may, might, should, must, will, would, have to, ought to*

- The music was very loud and **could be heard** from far away.
- I haven't received the letter. It **might have been sent** to the wrong address.

---

## 3. Passive with Two Objects (Cau bi dong voi 2 tan ngu)

Some verbs can have two objects: **give, offer, tell, show, ask, pay, teach, send**.

Active: Someone **gave me** a book.

Two possible passives:
- **I was given** a book. (more common -- person as subject)
- **A book was given** to me.

More examples:
- They **offered her** the job. --> **She was offered** the job.
- Someone **will show you** the way. --> **You will be shown** the way.
- They **paid him** very little. --> **He was paid** very little.

**Tip for exams:** In Vietnamese Grade 9 tests, the person-subject version is preferred and more commonly tested.

## 4. Passive with Make, Let, See, Hear (Dong tu chi giac va "make/let")

### Active: verb + object + bare infinitive
### Passive: verb + to-infinitive

| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| They **made** him **work** hard. | He **was made to work** hard. |
| I **saw** her **cross** the road. | She **was seen to cross** the road. |
| We **heard** him **sing** a song. | He **was heard to sing** a song. |

**Important:** In the passive, the bare infinitive changes to **to-infinitive**.

**Exception:** *let* is not normally used in the passive. Use **be allowed to** instead:
- They **let** us go home early. --> We **were allowed to go** home early.

### Perception verbs with -ing (action in progress)
- I **saw** him **crossing** the road. --> He **was seen crossing** the road.
- We **heard** them **talking**. --> They **were heard talking**.

## 5. Passive Reporting (Tuong thuat bi dong)

Used to report what people say, believe, think -- common in news and formal writing.

### Two structures:

**Structure 1: It + passive reporting verb + that-clause**
- **It is said that** he is 108 years old.
- **It is believed that** the boy is wearing a white sweater.
- **It is expected that** the strike will end soon.
- **It is reported that** two people were injured.

**Structure 2: Subject + passive reporting verb + to-infinitive**
- He **is said to be** 108 years old.
- The boy **is believed to be wearing** a white sweater.
- The strike **is expected to end** soon.
- Two people **are reported to have been injured**.

### Common reporting verbs:
*say, believe, think, know, expect, report, consider, allege, understand, suppose*

### To-infinitive forms in this structure:
| Time reference | Form | Example |
|----------------|------|---------|
| Present/future | to + V | He is said **to live** in France. |
| Continuous | to be + V-ing | She is believed **to be working** abroad. |
| Past | to have + V3 | He is reported **to have left** the country. |
| Past passive | to have been + V3 | The painting is believed **to have been stolen**. |

### "supposed to"
- The film **is supposed to be** good. (= people say it is good)
- He **is supposed to have robbed** a bank. (= people say he robbed a bank)

**Also:** "supposed to" can mean "expected/arranged":
- You **are supposed to be** at school. (= you should be)
- You **are not supposed to park** here. (= it is not allowed)

## 6. Have Something Done (Cau truc nho ai lam gi)

### Structure: **have + object + past participle**

This means you arrange for someone else to do something for you. You do not do it yourself.

| Situation | Sentence |
|-----------|----------|
| A painter painted her house. | She **had her house painted**. |
| A mechanic repaired my car. | I **had my car repaired**. |
| A hairdresser cut my hair. | I **had my hair cut**. |

### Tense variations:
| Tense | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Present simple | I **have** my car **serviced** every year. |
| Past simple | She **had** her eyes **tested** last week. |
| Present perfect | Have you ever **had** your house **broken into**? |
| Future | I'm going to **have** the kitchen **painted**. |
| Present continuous | She **is having** a new kitchen **installed**. |

### Get something done
You can also use **get** instead of **have** (more informal):
- I must **get** my phone **repaired**.
- When are you going to **get** the roof **fixed**?

### Have/get something done -- negative experience
Sometimes this structure describes something bad that happened (not arranged):
- Tom **had his car stolen** last week. (= someone stole his car)
- She **had her bag snatched**. (= someone snatched her bag)

## 7. Common Exam Patterns (Cac dang bai thuong gap)

### Pattern 1: Active to Passive transformation
- People say he is rich. --> **It is said that** he is rich. / **He is said to be** rich.

### Pattern 2: Causative (have/get something done)
- I asked someone to fix my computer. --> I **had my computer fixed**.

### Pattern 3: Passive with modals
- Someone must do it. --> It **must be done**.

### Pattern 4: Two-object passive
- They gave me a prize. --> I **was given** a prize.

### Pattern 5: Make/see/hear in passive
- They made us stay late. --> We **were made to stay** late.

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid (Loi thuong gap)

| Wrong | Correct | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| I am born in Hanoi. | I **was born** in Hanoi. | Birth = past event |
| He was made work hard. | He was made **to work** hard. | Passive of "make" needs "to" |
| The room has cleaned. | The room **has been cleaned**. | Need "been" in present perfect passive |
| I had the mechanic repaired my car. | I **had my car repaired**. | Object + past participle, not person |
| It is said that he is be rich. | It is said that he **is** rich. | No double "be" |
