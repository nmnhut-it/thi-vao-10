# Unit 40 - if I had known ... I wish I had known ...

## Section A - Third Conditional: if + had (done), ... would have (done)

Used to talk about the past (things that didn't happen):
- **If I'd known** you were in hospital, **I would have gone** to visit you. (= I didn't know, so I didn't go)

### More examples:
- I didn't see you when you passed me in the street. **If** I'**d seen** you, I would have said hello.
- They didn't go out last night. They would have gone out **if** they **hadn't been** so tired.
- **If** you'**d been looking** where you were going, you wouldn't have walked into the wall.
- The view was wonderful. I would have taken some pictures **if** I'**d had** a camera.

### Compare (present vs past):
- I'm not hungry. **If** I **was** hungry, I would eat something. (now)
- I wasn't hungry. **If** I **had been** hungry, I would have eaten something. (past)

## Section B - would not in the if-clause

Do NOT say 'if something would have happened'. Use **would** only in the other part:
- **If** I **had seen** you, I **would have said** hello. (*not* If I would have seen)

The short form **'d** can be either **would** or **had**:
- If I'**d** seen you (I'd seen = I **had** seen)
- I'**d** have said hello (I'd have said = I **would** have said)

## Section C - I wish + had done/known/been etc.

**wish + past perfect** = regret about something that happened (or didn't happen):
- I **wish** I'**d known** that Gary was ill. I would have gone to see him.
- I feel sick. I **wish** I **hadn't eaten** so much.
- Do you **wish** you'**d studied** science instead of languages?

### Compare:
- I'm glad I **saw** him. (= I saw him)
- I **wish** I'**d seen** him. (= I didn't see him)

**Note:** We do NOT say 'I wish ... would have ...':
- The weather was cold. I wish it **had been** warmer. (*not* I wish it would have been)

## Section D - Compare would (do) and would have (done)

- If I'd gone to the party last night, I **would be** tired now. (present result)
- If I'd gone to the party last night, I **would have met** lots of people. (past result)

### Compare would have, could have, and might have:
- If the weather hadn't been so bad, we **would have gone** out. (certain)
- If the weather hadn't been so bad, we **could have gone** out. (= would have been able to)
- If the weather hadn't been so bad, we **might have gone** out. (= maybe would have)
