# Unit 44 - Passive 3

## Section A - I was offered ... / we were given ... etc.

Some verbs can have two objects. For example, **give**:
- My grandfather gave **me** this watch. (object 1 = me, object 2 = this watch)

Two possible passive sentences:
- **I was given** this watch (by my grandfather).
- **This watch was given** to me (by my grandfather).

Other verbs with two objects: **ask, offer, pay, show, tell**

Most often we begin with the *person*:
- I'**ve been offered** the job, but I don't think I want it.
- **You will be given** plenty of time to decide.
- I didn't see the original document, but **I was shown** a copy.
- Tim has an easy job -- **he's paid a lot of money** to do very little.

## Section B - I don't like being ...

The passive of **doing/seeing** etc. is **being done/being seen** etc.

- Active: I don't like **people telling me** what to do.
- Passive: I don't like **being told** what to do.

### Examples:
- I remember **being taken** to the zoo when I was a child.
- Steve hates **being kept** waiting.
- We climbed over the wall without **being seen**.

## Section C - I was born ...

We say 'I **was** born ...' (*not* I am born):
- I **was born** in Chicago.
- Where **were** you **born**?

*but* for the present general: How many babies **are born** every day?

## Section D - get in passive

You can use **get** instead of **be** for the passive (informal):
- There was a fight, but nobody **got hurt**. (= nobody was hurt)
- I don't **get invited** to many parties. (= I'm not invited)
- I'm surprised Liz **didn't get offered** the job. (= wasn't offered)

### When to use get (not be):
- Only when things *happen* (not states): Jessica **is liked** by everybody. (*not* gets liked)
- Mainly informal spoken English; **be** works in all situations.

### Common expressions with get (not passive in meaning):
- **get married**, **get divorced**
- **get dressed** (= put on your clothes)
- **get lost** (= not know where you are)
- **get changed** (= change your clothes)
