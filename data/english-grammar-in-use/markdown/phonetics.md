# Phonetics (Ngu am)

## Ngu am: Quy tac phat am va trong am

---

## 1. Pronunciation of -s / -es Endings (Phat am duoi -s / -es)

The **-s / -es** ending is added to nouns (plural) and verbs (third person singular present). It has three possible pronunciations.

### 1.1 Three sounds: /s/, /z/, /ɪz/

| Sound | When to use | Final sound of base word | Examples |
|-------|-------------|------------------------|---------|
| **/s/** | After **voiceless** consonant sounds (am vo thanh) | /p/, /t/, /k/, /f/, /θ/ | cups, cats, books, laughs, months |
| **/z/** | After **voiced** consonant sounds and vowel sounds (am huu thanh va nguyen am) | /b/, /d/, /g/, /v/, /ð/, /m/, /n/, /ŋ/, /l/, /r/ and all vowels | jobs, beds, dogs, drives, rooms, pens, songs, balls, cars, days, trees, goes |
| **/ɪz/** | After **sibilant/hissing** sounds (am xit) | /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, /dʒ/ | buses, sizes, wishes, garages, watches, bridges |

### 1.2 How to remember (Cach ghi nho)

**Step 1:** Look at the **last sound** (not the last letter!) of the base word.

**For /ɪz/:** The base word ends in an "s-like" or "sh-like" sound. These are the letters/combinations:
- **-s** (bus -> buses), **-ss** (miss -> misses)
- **-z** (quiz -> quizzes)
- **-sh** (wash -> washes)
- **-ch** (watch -> watches)
- **-x** (box -> boxes) -- because x = /ks/
- **-ge/-ce** (change -> changes, place -> places)

**Memory trick for /ɪz/:** If the word ends in **s, z, sh, ch, x, ge, ce** -> pronounce /ɪz/ (them mot am tiet).

**For /s/:** The base word ends in a voiceless sound. Remember the phrase:
> **"Pet Cough"** -> /p/, /t/, /k/, /f/, /θ/
- stop**s**, cat**s**, cook**s**, belief**s**, mouth**s** (when th = /θ/)

**For /z/:** Everything else (all other sounds, including all vowel endings):
- dog**s**, live**s**, room**s**, tree**s**, boy**s**, play**s**

### 1.3 Practice examples

| Word | Last sound | -s pronunciation |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| map**s** | /p/ voiceless | /s/ |
| bed**s** | /d/ voiced | /z/ |
| watch**es** | /tʃ/ sibilant | /ɪz/ |
| love**s** | /v/ voiced | /z/ |
| lake**s** | /k/ voiceless | /s/ |
| bridge**s** | /dʒ/ sibilant | /ɪz/ |
| sea**s** | vowel | /z/ |
| laugh**s** | /f/ voiceless | /s/ |
| page**s** | /dʒ/ sibilant | /ɪz/ |
| month**s** | /θ/ voiceless | /s/ |

## 2. Pronunciation of -ed Endings (Phat am duoi -ed)

The **-ed** ending is added to regular verbs in the past tense and past participle. It has three possible pronunciations.

### 2.1 Three sounds: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/

| Sound | When to use | Final sound of base word | Examples |
|-------|-------------|------------------------|---------|
| **/t/** | After **voiceless** consonant sounds (am vo thanh) | /p/, /k/, /f/, /s/, /ʃ/, /tʃ/, /θ/ | stopped, cooked, laughed, missed, washed, watched, breathed* |
| **/d/** | After **voiced** consonant sounds and vowel sounds (am huu thanh va nguyen am) | /b/, /g/, /v/, /z/, /ʒ/, /dʒ/, /ð/, /m/, /n/, /ŋ/, /l/, /r/ and all vowels | robbed, begged, lived, closed, judged, breathed*, climbed, learned, played, enjoyed |
| **/ɪd/** | After **/t/** or **/d/** sounds | /t/, /d/ | wanted, needed, started, decided, hated, landed |

### 2.2 How to remember (Cach ghi nho)

**For /ɪd/:** This is the easiest -- only when the base verb ends in **/t/** or **/d/**:
- want**ed**, need**ed**, start**ed**, decid**ed**, hat**ed**, land**ed**, visit**ed**, wait**ed**, paint**ed**

**Memory trick:** If the base word already ends in a **t** or **d** sound, you need the extra syllable /ɪd/.

**For /t/:** After voiceless consonants (except /t/). Use the same "voiceless" concept as -s endings:
- stopp**ed** /stɒpt/, cook**ed** /kʊkt/, laugh**ed** /lɑːft/, miss**ed** /mɪst/, wash**ed** /wɒʃt/, watch**ed** /wɒtʃt/

**For /d/:** Everything else (voiced consonants and vowels):
- play**ed** /pleɪd/, open**ed** /ˈəʊpənd/, live**ed** /lɪvd/, enjoy**ed** /ɪnˈdʒɔɪd/

### 2.3 Practice examples

| Word | Last sound of base | -ed pronunciation |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| work**ed** | /k/ voiceless | /t/ |
| call**ed** | /l/ voiced | /d/ |
| want**ed** | /t/ | /ɪd/ |
| need**ed** | /d/ | /ɪd/ |
| stopp**ed** | /p/ voiceless | /t/ |
| enjoy**ed** | vowel /ɔɪ/ | /d/ |
| wash**ed** | /ʃ/ voiceless | /t/ |
| open**ed** | /n/ voiced | /d/ |
| paint**ed** | /t/ | /ɪd/ |
| chang**ed** | /dʒ/ voiced | /d/ |

## 3. Common Consonant Letter Pronunciations (Phat am phu am thong dung)

### 3.1 The letter C

| Sound | Rule | Examples |
|-------|------|---------|
| **/s/** (soft c) | Before **e, i, y** | **c**ity, **c**enter, **c**ycle, **c**inema, pri**c**e, fa**c**e |
| **/k/** (hard c) | Before **a, o, u** or consonants, and at end of words | **c**at, **c**ome, **c**up, **c**lass, musi**c**, publi**c** |

### 3.2 The letter G

| Sound | Rule | Examples |
|-------|------|---------|
| **/dʒ/** (soft g) | Before **e, i, y** (usually) | **g**eneral, **g**iant, **g**ym, sta**ge**, chan**ge** |
| **/g/** (hard g) | Before **a, o, u** or consonants | **g**ame, **g**o, **g**uitar, **g**reen, bi**g** |
| Exceptions | Some common words keep hard /g/ before e, i | **g**et, **g**ive, **g**irl, be**g**in, **g**ift, tar**g**et, for**g**et |

### 3.3 The letter S

| Sound | Position/Context | Examples |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| **/s/** | Beginning of words, after voiceless consonants | **s**un, **s**it, **s**top, de**s**k, mi**s**take |
| **/z/** | Between two vowels (often) | rea**s**on, mu**s**ic, ea**s**y, plea**s**e, vi**s**it |
| **/ʃ/** | Before -ure, -ion (sometimes) | **s**ure, pre**ss**ure, mis**s**ion |
| **/ʒ/** | Before -ion, -ure (sometimes) | vi**s**ion, plea**s**ure, mea**s**ure, deci**s**ion |

### 3.4 TH

| Sound | Examples | Vietnamese |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **/θ/** (voiceless) | **th**ink, **th**ank, **th**ree, tee**th**, ba**th**, heal**th**, mou**th** (noun) | Am vo thanh, dat luoi giua hai rang |
| **/ð/** (voiced) | **th**is, **th**at, **th**ere, **th**en, mo**th**er, bro**th**er, wea**th**er, wi**th** | Am huu thanh, dat luoi giua hai rang |

**Pattern:** Function words (this, that, the, there, they, them, than, though) usually have /ð/. Content words (think, thank, three, tooth, truth, thick, thin) usually have /θ/.

### 3.5 CH

| Sound | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **/tʃ/** (most common) | **ch**air, **ch**ildren, tea**ch**, lun**ch**, wat**ch** |
| **/k/** (Greek origin) | **ch**aracter, **ch**emistry, **ch**orus, s**ch**ool, stoma**ch**, te**ch**nology |
| **/ʃ/** (French origin) | **ch**ef, ma**ch**ine, bro**ch**ure, musta**ch**e |

## 4. Silent Letters (Am cam / chu cai khong phat am)

Many English words contain letters that are written but **not pronounced**.

| Silent letter | Examples | Note |
|---------------|---------|------|
| **k** before n | **k**now, **k**nife, **k**night, **k**nee, **k**nock, **k**nowledge | k cam truoc n |
| **w** before r | **w**rite, **w**rong, **w**rap, **w**rist, **w**reck | w cam truoc r |
| **b** after m | clim**b**, com**b**, lam**b**, bom**b**, thum**b**, dum**b** | b cam sau m |
| **b** before t | dou**b**t, de**b**t, su**b**tle | b cam truoc t |
| **g** before n | si**g**n, desi**g**n, forei**g**n, rei**g**n, campai**g**n | g cam truoc n |
| **h** at start (some words) | **h**our, **h**onest, **h**onour, **h**eir | h cam o dau tu |
| **l** in some words | wa**l**k, ta**l**k, ha**l**f, ca**l**m, sa**l**mon, cou**l**d, wou**l**d, shou**l**d | l cam |
| **t** in some words | lis**t**en, fas**t**en, cas**t**le, Chris**t**mas, of**t**en* | t cam |
| **p** before s, n | **p**sychology, **p**neumonia, recei**p**t | p cam |
| **n** after m | autum**n**, colum**n**, hym**n** | n cam sau m |

*Note: "often" can be pronounced with or without the /t/ sound -- both are accepted.

## 5. Word Stress: 2-Syllable Words (Trong am: Tu 2 am tiet)

### 5.1 General rules

| Word type | Stress position | Examples |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Nouns** (danh tu) | Usually on the **1st syllable** | **STU**dent, **TA**ble, **MO**ney, **WA**ter, **DOC**tor, **MU**sic |
| **Adjectives** (tinh tu) | Usually on the **1st syllable** | **HAP**py, **CLEV**er, **BU**sy, **USE**ful, **FA**mous |
| **Verbs** (dong tu) | Usually on the **2nd syllable** | be**GIN**, de**CIDE**, for**GET**, ar**RIVE**, en**JOY**, a**GREE** |

### 5.2 Words that are both noun and verb

Some words change stress depending on whether they are used as a noun or verb:

| Noun (stress on 1st) | Verb (stress on 2nd) | Vietnamese |
|---------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| **RE**cord | re**CORD** | ban ghi / ghi lai |
| **PRE**sent | pre**SENT** | qua / trinh bay |
| **OB**ject | ob**JECT** | doi tuong / phan doi |
| **PRO**duce | pro**DUCE** | san pham / san xuat |
| **CON**duct | con**DUCT** | cach xu su / dieu khien |
| **CON**flict | con**FLICT** | xung dot / mau thuan |
| **CON**trast | con**TRAST** | su tuong phan / doi chieu |
| **PER**mit | per**MIT** | giay phep / cho phep |
| **PRO**test | pro**TEST** | cuoc bieu tinh / phan doi |
| **EX**port | ex**PORT** | hang xuat khau / xuat khau |
| **IM**port | im**PORT** | hang nhap khau / nhap khau |

### 5.3 Common exceptions (Ngoai le)

Some 2-syllable verbs have stress on the **1st** syllable:
- **AN**swer, **EN**ter, **HAP**pen, **LIS**ten, **OF**fer, **O**pen, **VI**sit, **TRA**vel

## 6. Word Stress: 3-Syllable Words and Longer

### 6.1 General patterns

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| Stress on **1st** syllable | **BEAU**tiful, **EV**eryone, **EX**ercise, **IN**ternet, **EV**idence |
| Stress on **2nd** syllable | ex**PEN**sive, im**POR**tant, to**MOR**row, com**PU**ter, de**CI**sion |
| Stress on **3rd** syllable (from end) | under**STAND**, enter**TAIN**, volun**TEER** |

## 7. Suffix Stress Patterns (Quy tac trong am theo hau to)

This is the most reliable way to predict stress in longer words. Learn these suffix rules.

### 7.1 Suffixes that ATTRACT stress to themselves (Trong am roi vao am tiet chua hau to)

| Suffix | Stress on | Examples |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **-ee** | the suffix itself | employ**EE**, refu**GEE**, absen**TEE**, devo**TEE** |
| **-eer** | the suffix itself | volun**TEER**, engin**EER**, pi**ONEER** |
| **-ese** | the suffix itself | Vietnam**ESE**, Japan**ESE**, Chin**ESE** |
| **-ique/-esque** | the suffix itself | un**IQUE**, tech**NIQUE**, pictur**ESQUE** |
| **-ade** | the suffix itself | lemon**ADE**, par**ADE** |
| **-oo** | the suffix itself | sham**POO**, bam**BOO**, tab**OO** |
| **-ette** | the suffix itself | cass**ETTE**, cigar**ETTE** |
| **-oon** | the suffix itself | bal**LOON**, car**TOON**, aft**ER**noon |
| **-ain** (verbs) | the suffix itself | enter**TAIN**, main**TAIN**, ex**PLAIN** |

### 7.2 Suffixes that place stress on the SYLLABLE BEFORE them (Trong am roi vao am tiet truoc hau to)

| Suffix | Stress on syllable before | Examples |
|--------|--------------------------|---------|
| **-tion / -sion** | syllable before | edu**CA**tion, de**CI**sion, in**VEN**tion, commu**NI**cation |
| **-ic / -ical** | syllable before -ic | eco**NOM**ic, scien**TIF**ic, his**TOR**ic, e**LEC**tric |
| **-ity** | syllable before | a**BIL**ity, elec**TRIC**ity, na**TION**ality, person**AL**ity |
| **-ial / -ious / -eous** | syllable before | of**FI**cial, in**DUS**trial, mys**TE**rious, gor**GE**ous |
| **-ive** | syllable before | ex**PEN**sive, at**TRAC**tive, com**PET**itive |
| **-ence / -ance** | syllable before | **DIF**ference, im**POR**tance, per**FOR**mance |
| **-ous** | syllable before | **DAN**gerous, **FA**mous, **POI**sonous, con**TIN**uous |
| **-ogy / -ophy / -omy** | syllable before | bi**OL**ogy, phi**LOS**ophy, e**CON**omy, as**TRON**omy |
| **-graphy** | syllable before | pho**TOG**raphy, ge**OG**raphy, bi**OG**raphy |

### 7.3 Suffixes that do NOT change stress (Hau to khong doi trong am)

| Suffix | Stress stays the same | Examples |
|--------|----------------------|---------|
| **-ment** | de**VEL**op -> de**VEL**opment | **GOV**ernment, a**CHIEVE**ment |
| **-ness** | **HAP**py -> **HAP**piness | **KIND**ness, **DARK**ness |
| **-ful** | **BEAU**ty -> **BEAU**tiful | **CARE**ful, **HOPE**ful |
| **-less** | **CARE** -> **CARE**less | **HOME**less, **USE**less |
| **-ly** | **CARE**ful -> **CARE**fully | **SLOW**ly, **HAP**pily |
| **-er / -or** | **TEACH** -> **TEACH**er | **DRIV**er, **VIS**itor |
| **-able** | **COM**fort -> **COM**fortable | en**JOY**able, **REA**sonable |
| **-ize** | **MOD**ern -> **MOD**ernize | **OR**ganize, **RE**alize |
| **-fy** | **BEAU**ty -> **BEAU**tify | **SIMP**lify, **IDEN**tify |
| **-en** | **WIDE** -> **WID**en | **SHORT**en, **STRENGTH**en |

## 8. Quick Reference: Exam Patterns (Tham khao nhanh cho bai thi)

### -s/-es pronunciation decision tree:

```
Word ends in s, z, sh, ch, x, ge, ce sound?
  -> YES: /ɪz/ (buses, watches, pages)
  -> NO: Is the final sound voiceless? (p, t, k, f, th=/θ/)
      -> YES: /s/ (maps, cats, books)
      -> NO: /z/ (dogs, trees, plays)
```

### -ed pronunciation decision tree:

```
Word ends in /t/ or /d/ sound?
  -> YES: /ɪd/ (wanted, needed)
  -> NO: Is the final sound voiceless? (p, k, f, s, sh, ch, th=/θ/)
      -> YES: /t/ (stopped, cooked, washed)
      -> NO: /d/ (played, opened, lived)
```

### Stress decision for exam:

```
Does the word have -tion, -sion, -ic, -ity, -ial?
  -> YES: Stress the syllable BEFORE the suffix.
Does the word have -ee, -eer, -ese, -ette?
  -> YES: Stress the suffix itself.
Is it a 2-syllable noun/adjective?
  -> Usually stress the 1st syllable.
Is it a 2-syllable verb?
  -> Usually stress the 2nd syllable.
```

## 9. Common Exam Mistakes (Loi thuong gap trong bai thi)

1. Saying "watch**es**" as /s/ instead of **/ɪz/** -- it has the extra syllable.
2. Saying "cook**ed**" as two syllables /kʊk-ɪd/ instead of one syllable **/kʊkt/**.
3. Saying "want**ed**" as one syllable instead of two: /ˈwɒn-tɪd/ = **/ɪd/**.
4. Putting stress on the 2nd syllable of nouns like "**RE**cord" when used as a noun.
5. Putting stress on the 1st syllable of verbs like "de**CIDE**" -- it should be on the 2nd.
6. Forgetting that -tion/-sion words have stress on the syllable **before** the suffix: edu**CA**tion, not **E**ducation.
7. Pronouncing silent letters: saying the /k/ in "know" or the /w/ in "write".
