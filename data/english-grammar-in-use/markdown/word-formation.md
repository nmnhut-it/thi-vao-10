# Word Formation (Cau tao tu)

## Cau tao tu: Tien to, hau to va cach chuyen doi tu loai

---

## 1. Why Word Formation Matters (Tai sao cau tao tu quan trong)

In the Grade 9 exam (Thi vao 10), you often need to change a word from one part of speech to another. For example:

- "She is very **beauty**." -> Wrong! You need the adjective: "She is very **beautiful**."
- "He drives **care**." -> Wrong! You need the adverb: "He drives **carefully**."

Understanding **suffixes** (hau to) helps you form the correct word type: noun (danh tu), adjective (tinh tu), adverb (trang tu), or verb (dong tu).

---

### 2.2 Spelling changes to watch

- happy -> happi**ness** (y -> i before -ness)
- decide -> deci**sion** (de -> sion)
- invite -> invit**ation** (e dropped)
- able -> abil**ity** (le -> lity)

## 4. Adverb Suffixes (Hau to tao trang tu)

The most common way to form an adverb is to add **-ly** to an adjective.

### 4.1 Basic rule: Adjective + -ly = Adverb

| Adjective | Adverb | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| slow | **slowly** | He walks **slowly**. |
| careful | **carefully** | She drives **carefully**. |
| quick | **quickly** | They left **quickly**. |
| happy | **happily** | The children played **happily**. |
| easy | **easily** | She passed the exam **easily**. |
| beautiful | **beautifully** | She sings **beautifully**. |
| polite | **politely** | He spoke **politely**. |
| serious | **seriously** | She was **seriously** hurt. |

### 4.2 Spelling rules for -ly

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most adjectives: add **-ly** | slow -> slowly, quiet -> quietly |
| Adjectives ending in **-y**: change **y** to **i**, add **-ly** | happy -> happily, easy -> easily |
| Adjectives ending in **-le**: change **-le** to **-ly** | simple -> simply, gentle -> gently |
| Adjectives ending in **-ic**: add **-ally** | basic -> basically, automatic -> automatically |
| Exception | true -> truly, whole -> wholly |

### 4.3 Irregular adverbs (Trang tu bat quy tac)

| Adjective | Adverb | Note |
|-----------|--------|------|
| good | **well** | He speaks English **well**. *(not: goodly)* |
| fast | **fast** | She runs **fast**. *(not: fastly)* |
| hard | **hard** | He works **hard**. *(hardly = almost not)* |
| late | **late** | She arrived **late**. *(lately = recently)* |
| early | **early** | He woke up **early**. |

**Warning (Canh bao):**
- **hard** (adverb) = cham chi, manh. **hardly** = hau nhu khong.
  - He works **hard**. (Anh ay lam viec cham chi.) vs. He **hardly** works. (Anh ay hau nhu khong lam viec.)
- **late** (adverb) = muon. **lately** = gan day.
  - She came **late**. vs. I haven't seen her **lately**.

## 6. Common Prefixes (Tien to thong dung)

Prefixes (tien to) are added to the **beginning** of a word to change its meaning, often to the opposite.

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **un-** | not, opposite | happy -> **unhappy**, able -> **unable**, fair -> **unfair** |
| **im-/in-/il-/ir-** | not | possible -> **impossible**, correct -> **incorrect**, legal -> **illegal**, regular -> **irregular** |
| **dis-** | not, opposite | agree -> **disagree**, appear -> **disappear**, honest -> **dishonest** |
| **mis-** | wrongly | understand -> **misunderstand**, lead -> **mislead** |
| **re-** | again | write -> **rewrite**, build -> **rebuild**, use -> **reuse** |
| **over-** | too much | work -> **overwork**, eat -> **overeat** |
| **pre-** | before | view -> **preview**, school -> **preschool** |

### When to use im-, in-, il-, ir-:
- Before **m, p**: **im-** (impossible, impolite, immature)
- Before **l**: **il-** (illegal, illogical, illiterate)
- Before **r**: **ir-** (irregular, irresponsible, irrelevant)
- Other consonants: **in-** (incorrect, independent, invisible)

## 7. Position Rules (Vi tri cua tu loai trong cau)

Knowing **where** each word type goes in a sentence is just as important as knowing the suffixes.

### 7.1 Nouns (Danh tu)

Nouns can appear in these positions:

| Position | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Subject** (chu ngu) | **Education** is important. |
| **Object** (tan ngu) | I admire his **kindness**. |
| **After articles** (sau mao tu): a, an, the | The **pollution** is terrible. |
| **After adjectives** (sau tinh tu) | It was a difficult **decision**. |
| **After possessives** (sau tu so huu): my, his, her | His **achievement** impressed everyone. |
| **After prepositions** (sau gioi tu) | Thanks for your **assistance**. |
| **After "much/little/a lot of"** (for uncountable) | There is a lot of **information**. |

### 7.2 Adjectives (Tinh tu)

Adjectives can appear in these positions:

| Position | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Before a noun** (truoc danh tu) | She is a **beautiful** girl. |
| **After linking verbs** (sau dong tu noi): be, seem, look, feel, become, get, appear, taste, smell, sound | She **is beautiful**. The soup **tastes delicious**. He **looks tired**. |
| **After "very, quite, rather, so, too, extremely"** | It's **very important**. She is **too young**. |
| **After "make/keep/find + object"** | The news made her **happy**. I find English **interesting**. |

### 7.3 Adverbs (Trang tu)

Adverbs can appear in these positions:

| Position | What they modify | Example |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| **After a verb** (or before) | Modify the verb | She sings **beautifully**. He **quickly** left. |
| **Before an adjective** | Modify the adjective | She is **extremely** talented. |
| **Before another adverb** | Modify the adverb | He runs **incredibly** fast. |
| **At the beginning/end of a sentence** | Modify the whole sentence | **Unfortunately**, he failed. He failed, **unfortunately**. |

### 7.4 Verbs (Dong tu)

Verbs appear after the subject, in these patterns:

| Position | Example |
|----------|---------|
| After subject | They **modernized** the factory. |
| After modal verbs (can, should, must...) | We should **strengthen** our team. |
| After "to" (infinitive) | It's hard to **simplify** this problem. |

## 9. Exam Strategy (Chien luoc lam bai thi)

When you see a word formation question:

1. **Identify the position** in the sentence (after article? after very? before noun? after linking verb?).
2. **Determine the word type needed** (noun, adjective, adverb, or verb).
3. **Add the correct suffix** to form that word type.
4. **Check for negative meaning** -- do you need a prefix like un-, im-, dis-?
5. **Check spelling** -- watch for letter changes (y->i, e dropped, consonant doubled).

### Example exam questions:

1. She passed the exam **________**. (easy) -> Position after verb = adverb -> **easily**
2. He is a **________** driver. (care) -> Before noun = adjective -> **careful** or **careless** (check context)
3. The **________** of the air is a big problem. (pollute) -> After "the" = noun -> **pollution**
4. They were very **________** at the news. (surprise) -> After "very" = adjective -> **surprised**
5. We need to **________** the road. (wide) -> After "to" = verb -> **widen**

---

## 10. Common Exam Mistakes (Loi thuong gap trong bai thi)

1. **Wrong:** She is very *beauty*. **Right:** She is very **beautiful**. *(need adjective after "very")*
2. **Wrong:** He drives *careful*. **Right:** He drives **carefully**. *(need adverb to modify verb)*
3. **Wrong:** The *pollute* is terrible. **Right:** The **pollution** is terrible. *(need noun after "the")*
4. **Wrong:** It was a *successfully* project. **Right:** It was a **successful** project. *(need adjective before noun)*
5. **Wrong:** He is a *fame* singer. **Right:** He is a **famous** singer.
6. **Wrong:** She solved it *easy*. **Right:** She solved it **easily**.
