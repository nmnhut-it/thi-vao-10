# English Grammar Rules Summary for Grade 9 Exam Prep

Summarized from *English Grammar in Use* (5th Edition) by Raymond Murphy.

---

## 1. Verb Tenses (Units 1-25)

### Present Simple vs Present Continuous

- **Present simple** (I do): habits, routines, general truths, permanent situations. Signal words: always, usually, every day, often, never.
- **Present continuous** (I am doing): actions happening now or around now, temporary situations, changing situations (getting, increasing, improving).
- Stative verbs never take continuous: like, want, know, believe, understand, need, prefer, belong, contain, seem, mean, remember, realise, suppose, fit, consist.
- Exception: **think** can be continuous when it means "consider" (I'm thinking about it), but not when it means "believe" (I think he's right).
- **"I'm always doing"** expresses annoyance at repeated behavior (He's always losing his keys).

**Common mistake for Vietnamese learners:** Vietnamese has no verb conjugation, so students often forget the -s on third person singular (he works, NOT he work) or omit auxiliary do/does in questions.

### Past Simple vs Past Continuous

- **Past simple** (I did): completed actions in the past, sequences of events. Signal words: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2010.
- **Past continuous** (I was doing): action in progress at a specific past time, background action interrupted by another event.
- Key pattern: "When I arrived, they **were having** dinner" (already in progress) vs "When I arrived, they **had** dinner" (dinner happened after arrival).

### Present Perfect vs Past Simple

This is the most tested area in Vietnamese exams and the distinction that causes the most difficulty.

- **Present perfect** (I have done): past action with present relevance, unfinished time periods, experiences (ever/never), recent events (just, already, yet).
- **Past simple** (I did): finished time, specific past time mentioned.
- Use present perfect with: today, this week, this year, recently, so far, since, for, ever, never, just, already, yet.
- Use past simple with: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2005, when I was a child.
- **gone vs been**: "He has gone to Italy" = he is there now. "He has been to Italy" = he visited and came back.

**Key rule Vietnamese students miss:** You CANNOT use present perfect with a finished time expression. "I have seen him yesterday" is WRONG. Say "I saw him yesterday."

### Present Perfect Continuous (I have been doing)

- For activities that recently stopped or are still happening, especially with how long/for/since.
- Use continuous for *how long* (process): "How long have you been reading that book?"
- Use simple for *how many/how much* (completed): "How many pages have you read?"

### For and Since

- **for** + period of time (for two hours, for six months, for ages)
- **since** + starting point (since Monday, since 2001, since I arrived)
- Do NOT use present tense with for/since for duration: "I've known him for five years" (NOT I know him for five years).

**Critical for Vietnamese learners:** Vietnamese expresses duration differently. The English pattern "have + past participle + for/since" has no direct equivalent in Vietnamese grammar.

### Past Perfect (I had done)

- For actions completed before another past action: "When I arrived, the film **had** already **started**."
- The past perfect is the "past of the past" -- it establishes the earlier of two past events.
- Past perfect continuous (had been doing): emphasizes duration of activity before another past event.

### Future Forms

There are several ways to talk about the future, each with specific uses:

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **I'm doing** | Fixed arrangements | I'm meeting Kate tomorrow |
| **I'm going to do** | Decided intentions, predictions based on evidence | I'm going to buy a car. Look -- it's going to rain! |
| **will** | Instant decisions, predictions/beliefs, offers, promises | I'll help you. I think it will rain. |
| **Present simple** | Timetables, schedules | The train leaves at 9.30 |

- Do NOT use will for pre-arranged plans: "I'm meeting Kate tomorrow" (NOT I will meet).
- Do NOT use present tenses after if/when for future: "When I **arrive**" (NOT when I will arrive), "If it **rains**" (NOT if it will rain).

**Common Vietnamese exam mistake:** Using "will" everywhere for future. The exam tests whether students can distinguish between going to (intention/evidence) and will (spontaneous decision/prediction).

## 2. Modal Verbs (Units 26-37)

### Can, Could, Be able to

- **can**: present ability, possibility, permission
- **could**: past general ability, suggestions, less certain possibility
- **was/were able to** or **managed to**: specific past achievement (NOT could for one-time success). "The fire spread quickly, but everybody **was able to** escape" (NOT could escape).
- **could** for unrealistic situations: "I could sleep for a week" (= I'm very tired, not realistic)
- **could have done**: past possibility that didn't happen. "You could have stayed with us" (= but you didn't).

### Must and Can't (Deduction)

- **must** = I'm sure it's true: "You've been travelling all day. You must be tired."
- **can't** = I'm sure it's NOT true: "You've just had lunch. You can't be hungry."
- Past deduction: **must have done** / **can't have done**: "They must have gone out" (nobody answered the door).

### May and Might (Possibility)

- **may/might** = perhaps: "It may/might be true." Both are interchangeable for possibility.
- **may/might not** = perhaps not (different from can't which means impossible)
- **might** (not may) in unreal situations: "If they paid me better, I might work harder."
- Past: **may/might have done**: "She may have been asleep" (= perhaps she was asleep).
- **couldn't have** vs **might not have**: "She couldn't have received my message" (impossible) vs "She might not have received it" (possible she didn't).

### Have to vs Must

- **have to**: external obligation, facts. "I have to work from 8.30 to 5.30."
- **must**: speaker's opinion, rules/instructions. "You must keep it a secret."
- **mustn't** = don't do it (prohibition): "You mustn't tell anyone."
- **don't have to** = not necessary (no obligation): "You don't have to come with me."
- Past of must (obligation) = **had to**: "I had to leave early yesterday."

**Critical distinction for exams:** mustn't (prohibition) vs don't have to (no necessity). Vietnamese learners often confuse these because Vietnamese uses the same word for both concepts.

### Must, Mustn't, Needn't

- **needn't** = it's not necessary: "You needn't hurry, we have plenty of time."
- **needn't have done** = you did it, but it was unnecessary: "You needn't have rushed."
- **didn't need to** = it wasn't necessary (doesn't say whether you did it or not).

### Should and Ought to

- **should** = advice, opinion, expectation: "You should go to bed." "She should pass the exam."
- **should have done** = you didn't do it, but it would have been good: "You should have come to the party."
- **shouldn't have done** = you did it, but it was wrong: "I shouldn't have eaten so much."
- **ought to** = same as should (slightly more formal).
- After insist/suggest/recommend/demand: use **should** + infinitive or bare infinitive (subjunctive): "I suggested that he should apply" or "I suggested that he apply."

### Would

- For imaginary situations: "It would be nice to buy a new car, but we can't afford it."
- **would have done**: imaginary past. "I would have helped, but I was busy."
- **would** for past habits (= used to): "When I was young, we would go to the beach every summer."
- **wouldn't** = refused: "I tried to warn him, but he wouldn't listen."

### Had better and It's time

- **had better** (do): advice for a specific situation, implies danger if not followed. "You'd better hurry or you'll be late."
- **it's time** + past tense: "It's time we went home" (= we should go now). The past tense here does NOT refer to past time.

### Requests, Offers, Permission

- Requests: Can you / Could you / Would you (mind) ...?
- Permission: Can I / Could I / May I ...? (May is most formal)
- Offers: Can I / Shall I / Would you like ...?

## 3. Conditionals and Wish (Units 38-41)

### Type 1 (Real/Possible): If + present, will + infinitive

- For real or likely future situations: "If it rains, we'll stay at home."
- Use present tense (NOT will) in the if-clause: "If you **see** Sarah" (NOT if you will see).
- Can also use may/might/can instead of will: "If you need help, I **can** assist you."

### Type 2 (Unreal Present): If + past, would + infinitive

- For imaginary or unlikely present/future situations: "If I had more time, I would travel."
- Use past tense in the if-clause but the meaning is present/future, NOT past.
- Can use **were** instead of **was** after if (more formal): "If I were you" / "If I was you" (both OK).
- Can use could/might instead of would: "If it stopped raining, we could go out."

### Type 3 (Unreal Past): If + past perfect, would have + past participle

- For imaginary past situations (things that didn't happen): "If I had known, I would have told you."
- **would have done** = present result of imaginary past: "If I'd gone to the party, I would have met lots of people."
- Can use could have / might have: "If the weather hadn't been bad, we could have gone out."

### Mixed Conditionals (commonly tested)

- Type 3 condition + Type 2 result: "If I had studied harder (past), I would have a better job now (present)."

### Wish

- **wish + past simple**: regret about present situation. "I wish I knew the answer" (= I don't know it).
- **wish + past perfect**: regret about past. "I wish I had studied harder" (= I didn't study hard enough).
- **wish + would**: wanting something to change/happen or stop. "I wish it would stop raining." Used for complaints about annoying repeated behavior.
- Do NOT say "I wish I would": use "I wish I could" instead.
- **wish vs hope**: "I wish you luck" (= want), "I hope you enjoy" (NOT I wish you enjoy).

**Vietnamese exam trap:** The wish structure requires backshifting (past tense for present meaning). "I wish I am rich" is WRONG -- say "I wish I were/was rich."

## 4. Passive Voice (Units 42-46)

### Formation

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present simple | They clean the room | The room **is cleaned** |
| Past simple | They cleaned the room | The room **was cleaned** |
| Present continuous | They are cleaning it | It **is being cleaned** |
| Past continuous | They were cleaning it | It **was being cleaned** |
| Present perfect | They have cleaned it | It **has been cleaned** |
| Past perfect | They had cleaned it | It **had been cleaned** |
| Future (will) | They will clean it | It **will be cleaned** |
| Modal | They must clean it | It **must be cleaned** |
| Infinitive | They want to clean it | It wants **to be cleaned** |
| Perfect infinitive | They should have cleaned it | It **should have been cleaned** |

### Key Rules

- Use passive when the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious.
- Agent introduced by **by**: "This house was built **by** my grandfather."
- Verbs with two objects (give, offer, ask, tell, show, pay) can form two passives: "I was given a book" or "A book was given to me."
- **get + past participle** in informal speech: "He got hurt." "She got invited."
- We say "I was born" (NOT I am born) when talking about our birth.

### Special Passive Structures

- **It is said that... / He is said to...**: "It is said that he is 108 years old" = "He is said to be 108 years old." Works with: believed, expected, reported, known, alleged, thought.
- **supposed to**: "You're supposed to be on holiday" (= expected/arranged). "You're not supposed to park here" (= not allowed).
- **have something done**: "I had my hair cut" (= someone cut it for me, I arranged it). Order: have + object + past participle.

**Vietnamese exam note:** Vietnamese has no passive morphology, so students must memorize the be + past participle pattern. The most common error is forgetting to change the verb form or omitting "be."

## 5. Reported Speech (Units 47-48)

### Backshifting Rules

When the reporting verb is past (said, told), tenses shift back:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|--------------|-----------------|
| am/is | was |
| are | were |
| do/does | did |
| have/has | had |
| will | would |
| can | could |
| did / was / were | had done / had been |
| have done | had done |

### Key Points

- **said** vs **told**: "He said (that) he was ill." / "He told **me** (that) he was ill." (told needs an object)
- You can keep the original tense if the situation is still true: "He said his new job **is** boring" (if it's still boring).
- If the situation has changed, you MUST use past: "Rachel said you **were** in hospital" (but you're clearly not now).
- Commands/requests: told + object + to infinitive: "The doctor told me to drink plenty of water."
- Negative commands: told + object + not to: "I told Joe not to work too hard."
- Requests with ask: "She asked me to help her."

### Reported Questions

- Word order changes to statement order (no inversion): "Where are you going?" becomes "She asked where I was going" (NOT where was I going).
- Yes/no questions use **if** or **whether**: "Do you have a licence?" becomes "She asked if/whether I had a licence."
- Do NOT use do/does/did in reported questions: "What time does the film start?" becomes "I asked what time the film started" (NOT what time did the film start).

**Critical for Vietnamese exams:** Reported questions are heavily tested. Remember: no question mark, no inversion, no do/does/did auxiliary in the reported version.

## 6. Relative Clauses (Units 92-96)

### Relative Pronouns

| Pronoun | Use |
|---------|-----|
| **who** | people (subject) |
| **which** | things (subject/object) |
| **that** | people or things (subject/object) -- in defining clauses only |
| **whose** | possession (= his/her/its/their) |
| **whom** | people (object, formal) |
| **where** | places |

### Defining vs Non-Defining Clauses

**Defining (Type 1):** Essential information, no commas. Can use who/which/that. Can omit who/which/that when it is the object.
- "The woman **who lives next door** is a doctor." (subject -- cannot omit)
- "The book **(that) you lent me** was interesting." (object -- can omit that)
- "The hotel **(that) we stayed at** was nice." (preposition at end)

**Non-defining (Type 2):** Extra information, WITH commas. Use who/which only (NOT that). Cannot omit the pronoun.
- "My brother Ben, **who lives in Hong Kong**, is an architect."
- "Anna told me about her new job, **which** she's enjoying a lot."

### Important Details

- **whose**: "I met a man **whose** sister knows you" (= his sister).
- Do not confuse **whose** (possessive) and **who's** (= who is/who has).
- **where** replaces in which/at which: "The restaurant **where** we had lunch..."
- **the day/the time/the reason**: "That's **the reason** I'm calling you" or "the reason **why** I'm calling."
- In non-defining clauses, **which** can refer to a whole clause: "Joe got the job, **which** surprised everybody."
- Quantifiers with of whom/of which: "He has three cars, **none of which** he uses regularly."

**Vietnamese exam note:** The defining vs non-defining distinction (with/without commas) is critical. Vietnamese has no equivalent structure, so students must learn when commas are required.

## 7. Articles (Units 69-78)

### A/An (Indefinite Article)

- Use with singular countable nouns mentioned for the first time or when talking about what kind: "That's **a** nice table."
- Use for jobs: "Sandra is **a** nurse."
- Use for rates: "once **a** week," "three times **a** day," "1.50 **a** kilo."
- Do NOT use with uncountable nouns: "I need information" (NOT an information).

### The (Definite Article)

Use **the** when:
- There is only one (or it's clear which one): "the sun," "the door," "the manager."
- Second mention: "I saw **a** dog and **a** cat. **The** dog was chasing **the** cat."
- With superlatives: "the best," "the longest river."
- With "same": "the same colour."
- Unique things: the world, the sky, the sea, the ground, the earth, the moon.
- Types/inventions: the giraffe (= giraffes as a species), the telephone, the piano.
- Nationality adjectives ending in -ch, -sh, -ese, -ss: the French, the English, the Chinese.
- The + adjective for groups: the old, the rich, the poor, the homeless, the injured.
- Geographic names: oceans/seas/rivers/canals (the Atlantic, the Nile), deserts (the Sahara), plural countries/mountain ranges/island groups (the Netherlands, the Alps, the Canaries).

### No Article (Zero Article)

Do NOT use the with:
- General/abstract concepts: "Life is short." "Music makes me happy." "Children learn quickly."
- Most country/city/continent names: France, Tokyo, Africa.
- Single mountains/islands/lakes: Mount Everest, Lake Victoria.
- Meals (general): breakfast, lunch, dinner (but: "a big lunch," "the lunch we had").
- Languages, sports, academic subjects: English, football, history.
- bed/work/home/school/hospital/prison/church/university (when used for their primary purpose): "go to school" (as a student) vs "go to the school" (visit the building).
- TV (but: the radio): "I watch TV" vs "I listen to the radio."
- Next/last + day/week/month: "next Friday" (NOT the next Friday), "last June."

### Countable vs Uncountable

Common uncountable nouns (no plural, no a/an): accommodation, advice, baggage, behaviour, bread, chaos, damage, furniture, information, luggage, luck, news, permission, progress, scenery, traffic, weather, work.

**Vietnamese exam note:** Vietnamese has no articles at all, making this one of the hardest topics. The most common errors are omitting "the" when needed and adding "the" before general concepts. Pay special attention to the school/hospital/prison pattern.

## 8. Prepositions (Units 121-136)

### Time Prepositions: at/on/in

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|------------|-----|---------|
| **at** | Clock times, specific moments | at 5 o'clock, at midnight, at lunchtime, at the moment, at Christmas, at night |
| **on** | Days, dates | on Friday, on 16 May, on New Year's Day, on my birthday, on Monday morning |
| **in** | Longer periods | in June, in 2012, in the 1990s, in winter, in the morning, in the past |

- No preposition before **last/next/this/every**: "See you next Friday" (NOT on next Friday).
- **in** + future time period: "The train will leave **in** a few minutes" (= a few minutes from now).
- **on time** = punctual. **in time** = soon enough, not late. **at the end** (of something) = at the time when it ends. **in the end** = finally.

### Position Prepositions: in/at/on

- **in**: enclosed spaces (in a room, in a garden, in a town, in a country, in the water, in the sky).
- **at**: points/locations (at the bus stop, at the door, at the roundabout, at the top/bottom/end).
- **on**: surfaces (on the wall, on the floor, on a page, on an island, on a river/coast/road).
- **in** vs **at** for buildings: "in the shop" (inside) vs "at the shop" (at that location); "in Helen's house" (inside the building) vs "at Helen's house" (at her place).
- Cities/countries use **in**: "in Paris," "in France." Points on a journey use **at**: "Does this train stop at Oxford?"
- Transport: **on** a bus/train/plane/bike BUT **in** a car/taxi.

### Places without "the" + preposition patterns

- **at** school/home/work/university (for primary purpose)
- **in** bed/hospital/prison (for primary purpose)
- **go to** + place (movement), **be at/in** (position)
- **go home / come home / arrive home** (no preposition): NOT go to home.
- **arrive in** + city/country, **arrive at** + other places: "arrive in London," "arrive at the hotel."

### By

- Means of transport (no article): **by** car/bus/train/plane/bike BUT **in** my car, **on** the bus.
- Agent in passive: "painted **by** Picasso."
- Method: **by** hand, **by** email, **by** credit card, **by** mistake, **by** chance, **by** accident.
- Contrast: **on purpose** (NOT by purpose).
- Deadline: "**by** Friday" = not later than Friday. Compare: **until** (continuing up to) vs **by** (completed before).
- **by** for difference: "won **by** five metres."

### Noun + Preposition Collocations

- a reason **for**, a cause **of**, a picture **of**, an advantage **of**, a solution **to**, a key **to**, an answer **to**, a reply **to**, a reaction **to**, a demand **for**, a need **for**, damage **to**, a relationship **with**, a connection **between**, an increase/decrease **in**, an attitude **to/towards**.

### Adjective + Preposition (most commonly tested)

- afraid **of**, interested **in**, good/bad **at**, responsible **for**, similar **to**, different **from**, married **to**, angry **with** (a person), angry **about** (a situation), sorry **for/about**, pleased/satisfied **with**, tired **of**, keen **on**, fond **of**, proud **of**, famous **for**, dependent **on**.

### Verb + Preposition (most commonly tested)

- listen **to**, look **at**, wait **for**, ask **for**, belong **to**, depend **on**, consist **of**, succeed **in**, insist **on**, apologize **for**, blame (somebody) **for**, congratulate (somebody) **on**, accuse (somebody) **of**, complain **about/to**, dream **about/of**, think **about/of**, hear **about/from**, remind (somebody) **of**.

**Vietnamese exam note:** Preposition collocations must be memorized. There is no logical system -- they are arbitrary. Vietnamese preposition usage is very different, so translation-based guessing will fail. Focus on the most common exam collocations listed above.

## Quick Reference: Most Common Exam Traps

1. **Present perfect vs Past simple**: Never use present perfect with yesterday/ago/last week.
2. **Mustn't vs Don't have to**: Prohibition vs No necessity.
3. **If + will**: Never use will in the if-clause for conditionals.
4. **Wish + past tense**: Always backshift after wish.
5. **Reported questions**: No inversion, no do/does/did.
6. **Relative clauses**: That CANNOT be used in non-defining (comma) clauses.
7. **Articles with school/hospital**: No article for primary purpose, "the" for visiting.
8. **For vs Since**: For + duration, Since + starting point.
9. **Used to vs Be used to**: "I used to live" (past habit) vs "I'm used to living" (accustomed to).
10. **Say vs Tell**: Tell always needs a person object. Say does not.
