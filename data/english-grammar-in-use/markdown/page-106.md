# Unit 47 - Reported speech 1 (he said that ...)

## A: Direct vs Reported speech

Two ways to report what someone said:
- **Direct speech:** Paul said, '**I'm feeling ill.**'
- **Reported speech:** Paul said **that he was feeling ill.**

Changes: **I** → **he**, **am** → **was**

## B: Tense changes in reported speech

The main verb of the sentence is usually past (said/told). The rest of the sentence is usually past too:
- Paul **said** that he **was feeling** ill.
- I **told** Lisa that I **didn't have** any money.

You can leave out **that**: Paul **said** he was feeling ill.

### Tense shift rules:

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| am/is → **was** | do/does → **did** |
| are → **were** | have/has → **had** |
| will → **would** | can → **could** |
| want/like/know/go etc. → **wanted/liked/knew/went** etc. |

### Examples (Anna's direct speech → reported speech):
- 'I**'ve** lost my phone.' → She said she **had** lost her phone.
- 'I **want** to buy a car.' → She said she **wanted** to buy a car.
- 'I **can't** come to the party on Friday.' → She said she **couldn't** come to the party on Friday.
- 'I **don't** have much free time.' → She said she **didn't** have much free time.
- 'My parents **are** fine.' → She said her parents **were** fine.
- 'I**'m** going away for a few days.' → She said she **was** going away for a few days.
- 'I**'ll** phone you when I **get** back.' → She said she **would** phone me when she **got** back.

## C: Past simple can stay the same or change to past perfect

- Paul said: 'I **woke** up feeling ill, so I **didn't go** to work.'
- Reported: Paul said he **woke/had woken** up feeling ill, so he **didn't go/hadn't gone** to work.
