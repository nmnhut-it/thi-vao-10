# Reported Speech - Cau tuong thuat (Loi noi gian tiep)

## Gioi thieu (Introduction)

When we tell someone what another person said, we can:
- **Repeat the exact words** (direct speech / loi noi truc tiep): Paul said, "I am feeling ill."
- **Report what was said** (reported speech / loi noi gian tiep): Paul said that he was feeling ill.

In reported speech, we usually change tenses, pronouns, and time expressions because we are reporting from a different time and perspective.

---

## 1. Bang lui thi (Tense Backshifting Table)

When the reporting verb is in the past (said, told, asked...), we normally shift the tense back:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---|---|
| Present simple: "I **work** hard." | Past simple: She said she **worked** hard. |
| Present continuous: "I **am working**." | Past continuous: She said she **was working**. |
| Past simple: "I **worked** late." | Past perfect: She said she **had worked** late. |
| Present perfect: "I **have finished**." | Past perfect: She said she **had finished**. |
| Past continuous: "I **was working**." | Past perfect cont.: She said she **had been working**. |
| **will** | **would** |
| **can** | **could** |
| **may** | **might** |
| **must** (obligation) | **had to** |
| **shall** | **should / would** |

### Cac thay doi khac (Other changes):

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| I / me / my | he, she / him, her / his, her |
| we / us / our | they / them / their |
| you | I, he, she, they (tuy nguu canh) |
| this | that |
| these | those |
| here | there |
| now | then / at that time |
| today | that day |
| tonight | that night |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| ago | before / earlier |
| last week | the week before / the previous week |
| next week | the following week |

### Vi du day du (Full examples):

> Direct: Anna said, "I have lost my phone."
> Reported: Anna said (that) she **had** lost **her** phone.

> Direct: Tom said, "I will call you tomorrow."
> Reported: Tom said (that) he **would** call me **the next day**.

> Direct: She said, "I am going away for a few days."
> Reported: She said (that) she **was** going away for a few days.

**Luu y:** "That" co the luoc bo (optional): Paul said **(that)** he was feeling ill.

---

## 2. Khi nao KHONG can lui thi (When NOT to Backshift)

### a) Tinh huong van dung (The situation is still the same)

If what the person said is still true now, you can keep the original tense:

> Direct: Paul said, "My new job **is** boring."
> Reported: Paul said his new job **is** boring. (still boring now)
> OR: Paul said his new job **was** boring. (also correct)

### b) Chan ly, su that khoa hoc (Universal truths)

> The teacher said that the Earth **goes** round the Sun. (always true)
> She told me that water **boils** at 100 degrees. (scientific fact)

### c) Vua moi noi xong (Just-said statements)

When you report something immediately or very recently, backshifting is optional:

> Anna says she **is** coming to the party. (reporting verb in present - no change needed)
> Anna said she **is** coming to the party. (also acceptable when still relevant)

### d) Past simple co the giu nguyen hoac lui thi

> Direct: Paul said, "I **woke** up feeling ill, so I did not go to work."
> Reported: Paul said he **woke** up feeling ill, so he did not go to work. (OK)
> OR: Paul said he **had woken** up feeling ill, so he had not gone to work. (also OK)

### e) Modal verbs da o dang qua khu

**Would, could, might, should, ought to, had better, used to, needn't** do NOT change:
> She said she **would** help me. (no change needed)

## 3. Say vs Tell - Phan biet "say" va "tell"

### TELL = noi voi ai do (always needs a person)

- Rachel **told me** that you were in hospital. (NOT: Rachel told that...)
- What did you **tell the police**? (NOT: say the police)

### SAY = noi (does NOT need a person directly after it)

- Rachel **said** that you were in hospital. (NOT: Rachel said me...)
- What did you **say**?

### SAY something TO somebody

- Anna **said goodbye to** me and left. (NOT: Anna said me goodbye)
- What did you **say to** the police?

### Tom tat:

| Dung | Sai |
|---|---|
| He **said** (that) he was tired. | He said me he was tired. |
| He **told me** (that) he was tired. | He told that he was tired. |
| She **said to** me that... | She said me that... |

## 4. Dong tu tuong thuat (Reporting Verbs)

Ngoai **say** va **tell**, tieng Anh co nhieu dong tu tuong thuat khac, moi dong tu co cau truc rieng. Day la nhung dong tu thuong gap trong de thi vao 10:

### a) Verb + to-infinitive

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **agree** | S + agreed + to V | He **agreed to help** me. |
| **offer** | S + offered + to V | She **offered to drive** me home. |
| **promise** | S + promised + to V | Tom **promised to call** me. |
| **refuse** | S + refused + to V | She **refused to answer**. |
| **threaten** | S + threatened + to V | He **threatened to call** the police. |

### b) Verb + object + to-infinitive

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **advise** | S + advised + O + to V | The doctor **advised me to rest**. |
| **ask** | S + asked + O + to V | She **asked me to help** her. |
| **encourage** | S + encouraged + O + to V | He **encouraged me to apply**. |
| **invite** | S + invited + O + to V | They **invited us to come**. |
| **order** | S + ordered + O + to V | The officer **ordered them to stop**. |
| **remind** | S + reminded + O + to V | She **reminded me to lock** the door. |
| **tell** | S + told + O + to V | The doctor **told me to drink** plenty of water. |
| **warn** | S + warned + O + (not) to V | She **warned me not to touch** it. |

### c) Verb + V-ing

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **admit** | S + admitted + V-ing | He **admitted stealing** the money. |
| **deny** | S + denied + V-ing | She **denied breaking** the window. |
| **suggest** | S + suggested + V-ing | Tom **suggested going** to the cinema. |
| **apologize for** | S + apologized for + V-ing | He **apologized for being** late. |

### d) Suggest - cac cau truc dac biet

**Suggest** co nhieu cach dung:
- S + suggested + **V-ing**: He suggested **going** out for dinner.
- S + suggested + **that + S + (should) + V**: He suggested that we **(should) go** out.
- S + suggested + **that + S + V (subjunctive)**: He suggested that she **be** more careful.

**Luu y:** KHONG dung "He suggested me to go." hoac "He suggested to go."

## 5. Tuong thuat cau hoi (Reported Questions)

### Quy tac quan trong: KHONG dao ngu trong cau hoi gian tiep

In reported questions, we use **statement word order** (S + V), NOT question word order.

### a) Yes/No questions -> if / whether

> Direct: "Do you speak English?" she asked me.
> Reported: She asked me **if/whether** I spoke English.

> Direct: "Have you been to Paris?"
> Reported: He asked me **if/whether** I had been to Paris.

### b) Wh- questions -> giu tu hoi, bo do ngu

> Direct: "Where do you live?" she asked.
> Reported: She asked me **where** I lived. (NOT: where did I live)

> Direct: "What time does the train leave?"
> Reported: I asked **what time** the train left.

> Direct: "Why did you not call me?"
> Reported: She asked me **why** I had not called her.

### Tom tat cau truc:

```
Direct Yes/No:  "Do/Does/Did + S + V...?"
Reported:        S + asked (O) + if/whether + S + V(lui thi)

Direct Wh-:     "Wh- + do/does/did + S + V...?"
Reported:        S + asked (O) + Wh- + S + V(lui thi)
```

**Loi thuong gap:**

| Sai | Dung |
|---|---|
| She asked where did I live. | She asked where I **lived**. |
| He asked did I like coffee. | He asked **if/whether** I liked coffee. |
| They asked what was my name. | They asked what my **name was**. |

---

## 6. Tuong thuat cau menh lenh va yeu cau (Reported Commands and Requests)

### Cau menh lenh (Commands): tell + O + (not) to V

> Direct: "Drink plenty of water," the doctor said to me.
> Reported: The doctor **told me to drink** plenty of water.

> Direct: "Do not work too hard," I said to Joe.
> Reported: I **told Joe not to work** too hard.

### Cau yeu cau (Requests): ask + O + (not) to V

> Direct: "Can you help me, please?" Jackie said to me.
> Reported: Jackie **asked me to help** her.

> Direct: "Please do not make so much noise," she said.
> Reported: She **asked me not to make** so much noise.

### Cau truc chung:

```
Command:  S + told + O + (not) + to V
Request:  S + asked + O + (not) + to V
```

You can also use **said (not) to**:
> Paul **said not to worry** about him.

## 7. Mau chuyen doi thuong gap trong de thi vao 10 (Exam Transformation Patterns)

### Pattern 1: Cau tran thuat co ban
```
"I am tired," she said.
-> She said (that) she was tired.
```

### Pattern 2: Cau hoi Yes/No
```
"Are you happy?" he asked me.
-> He asked me if/whether I was happy.
```

### Pattern 3: Cau hoi Wh-
```
"Where did you go?" she asked.
-> She asked (me) where I had gone.
```

### Pattern 4: Cau menh lenh / yeu cau
```
"Open the door," he said to me.
-> He told me to open the door.

"Please do not go," she said.
-> She asked me not to go.
```

### Pattern 5: Dong tu tuong thuat dac biet
```
"I will help you," Tom said.
-> Tom offered to help me. / Tom promised to help me.

"You should see a doctor," she said to me.
-> She advised me to see a doctor.

"Do not touch that wire!" he said to us.
-> He warned us not to touch that wire.

"Let us go swimming," he said.
-> He suggested going swimming.

"I am sorry I am late," she said.
-> She apologized for being late.

"I did not break the window," he said.
-> He denied breaking the window.
```

### Pattern 6: Cau dieu kien / Wish trong tuong thuat
```
"I wish I had more time," she said.
-> She said she wished she had more time. (khong doi)

"If I were you, I would study harder," he said.
-> He advised me to study harder. (cach viet gon)
```

## 8. Loi thuong gap (Common Mistakes)

| Sai | Dung | Giai thich |
|---|---|---|
| She said me that... | She **told me** that... | "Say" khong di truc tiep voi nguoi |
| He asked where did I live. | He asked where I **lived**. | Khong dao ngu trong cau hoi gian tiep |
| He suggested me to go. | He **suggested going**. | "Suggest" khong dung O + to V |
| She told that she was tired. | She **told me** that she was tired. | "Tell" luon can tan ngu (nguoi) |
| He said he will come. | He said he **would** come. | will -> would khi lui thi |
| They asked me that I was OK. | They asked me **if** I was OK. | Cau hoi Yes/No dung if/whether |

## 9. Bai tap nhanh (Quick Practice)

**Chuyen sang loi noi gian tiep:**

1. "I am working tomorrow," said Tom.
   -> Tom said (that) he **was working** the next day.

2. "Do you like your new job?" I asked Sarah.
   -> I asked Sarah **if/whether she liked** her new job.

3. "Where have you been?" my mother asked me.
   -> My mother asked me **where I had been**.

4. "Do not forget to lock the door," she told me.
   -> She **reminded me to lock** the door.

5. "Let us have a picnic," said Linda.
   -> Linda **suggested having** a picnic.

6. "I did not take the money," the boy said.
   -> The boy **denied taking** the money.

7. "You should exercise more," the doctor said to me.
   -> The doctor **advised me to exercise** more.
