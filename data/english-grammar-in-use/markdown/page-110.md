# Unit 49 - Questions 1

## A: Word order in questions

In questions the subject is usually after the first verb:
- **subject + verb** → **verb + subject**
- Tom will → **Will Tom** be here tomorrow?
- you have → **Have you** been working hard?
- the house was → When **was the house** built?

The subject is after the **first** verb:
- **Is** Katherine working today? *(not Is working Katherine)*

## B: do/does in present simple, did in past simple

**Present simple** questions use **do/does**:
- you live → **do** you **live**? → **Do** you **live** near here?
- the film starts → **does** the film **start**? → What time **does** the film **start**?

**Past simple** questions use **did**:
- you sold → **did** you **sell**? → **Did** you **sell** your car?
- the train stopped → **did** the train **stop**? → Why **did** the train **stop**?

### Exception: when who/what is the subject

Do **not** use do/does/did if who/what etc. is the **subject** of the sentence:

| who as object | who as subject |
|---|---|
| Emma phoned **somebody**. → **Who** did Emma phone? | **Somebody** phoned Emma. → **Who** phoned Emma? |

When who/what etc. is the **subject**:
- **Who wants** something to eat? *(not Who does want)*
- **What happened** to you last night? *(not What did happen)*
- **How many people came** to the party? *(not did come)*
- **Which bus goes** to the centre? *(not does go)*

## C: Prepositions in questions

In questions beginning who/what/which/where, prepositions usually go at the end:
- **Where** are you **from**?
- **What** was the weather **like**?
- **Which** job has Tina applied **for**?

Formal style: preposition + **whom**: **To whom** do you wish to speak?

## D: Negative questions (isn't it ...? / didn't you ...?)

Used to show **surprise** or when we expect the listener to **agree**:
- **Didn't you** hear the doorbell? I rang it three times.
- **Haven't we** met before?

**Yes/No** answers to negative questions:
- 'Don't you want to go?' → '**Yes.**' (= I want to go) / '**No.**' (= I don't want to go)

Negative questions with **Why**:
- **Why don't we** eat out tonight?
- **Why wasn't** Emma at work yesterday?
