# Unit 41 - wish

## Section A - wish someone luck/success etc.

- I **wish you all the best** in the future.
- He **wished me luck**.

We say 'wish somebody something' (luck, happiness etc.), but NOT 'I wish something happens'. Use **hope** instead:
- I'm sorry you're not well. I **hope** you **feel** better soon. (*not* I wish you feel)

### Compare I wish and I hope:
- I **wish** you **a pleasant stay** at this hotel. (wish + noun phrase)
- I **hope** you **enjoy** your stay at this hotel. (hope + clause)

## Section B - I wish + past (present regret)

**wish + past** = regret about the present:
- I **wish** I **knew** what to do about the problem. (but I don't know)
- I **wish** you **didn't** have to go so soon. (but you have to)
- Do you **wish** you **lived** near the sea?
- I bought these shoes, but now I don't like them. I **wish** I **hadn't bought** them.

### wish + had known/said (past regret):
- I **wish** I'**d known** about the party. (but I didn't know)
- I **wish** I **hadn't said** it. (but I said it)

## Section C - I wish I could

**I wish I could (do)** = I regret that I cannot do it:
- I'm sorry you have to go. I wish you **could stay** longer.
- I wish I **could remember** his name.

**I wish I could have (done)** = I regret that I could not do it:
- I hear the party was great. I wish I **could have gone**.

## Section D - I wish ... would

**I wish something would happen** = I would like it to happen (but it probably won't):
- It's been raining all day. I **wish** it **would stop** raining.

Used to complain about a situation:
- The phone has been ringing for five minutes. I **wish** somebody **would answer** it.
- I **wish** you'**d do** something instead of just sitting and doing nothing.

**I wish ... wouldn't** = complain about things people do repeatedly:
- I **wish** you **wouldn't keep** interrupting me.

## Section E - I wish ... would vs I wish ... was/were

### Important distinction:
- **I wish ... would** = I want something to *happen* (an action/change): I **wish** Sarah **would come**. (= I want her to come)
- **I wish ... was/were** = I want something to *be* (a state): I **wish** Sarah **was** (or **were**) here now. (*not* I wish Sarah would be)

- I **wish** somebody **would buy** me a car. (action)
- I **wish** I **had** a car. (*not* I wish I would have) (state)
