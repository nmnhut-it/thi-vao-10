# Unit 43 - Passive 2 (be done / been done / being done)

## Section A - Passive Infinitive: (to) be done

- Active: Somebody **will clean** this room later.
- Passive: This room **will be cleaned** later.

### Examples:
- The situation is serious. Something must **be done** before it's too late.
- A mystery is something that can't **be explained**.
- The music was very loud and could **be heard** from a long way away.
- A new supermarket is going **to be built** next year.
- Please go away. I want **to be left** alone.

## Section B - Perfect Infinitive Passive: (to) have been done

- Active: Somebody **should have cleaned** the room.
- Passive: The room **should have been cleaned**.

### Examples:
- I haven't received the letter yet. It might **have been sent** to the wrong address.
- If you had locked the car, it wouldn't **have been stolen**.
- There were some problems at first, but they seem **to have been solved**.

## Section C - Present Perfect Passive: have/has been done

- Active: Somebody **has cleaned** it. -> Passive: It **has been cleaned**.

### Examples:
- Have you heard? The trip **has been cancelled**.
- Have you ever **been bitten** by a dog?
- 'Are you going to the party?' 'No, I **haven't been invited**.'

### Past Perfect Passive: had been done
- Active: Somebody **had cleaned** it. -> Passive: It **had been cleaned**.
- The vegetables didn't taste good. They **had been cooked** too long.
- The car was three years old, but **hadn't been used** very much.

## Section D - Continuous Passive: am/is/are being done / was/were being done

### Present Continuous Passive:
- Active: Somebody **is cleaning** the room. -> Passive: The room **is being cleaned**.
- There's somebody walking behind us. I think we **are being followed**.
- A new bridge **is being built** across the river.

### Past Continuous Passive:
- Active: Somebody **was cleaning** the room. -> Passive: The room **was being cleaned** when I arrived.
- There was somebody walking behind us. I think we **were being followed**.
