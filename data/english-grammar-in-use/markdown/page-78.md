# Unit 33 - should 1

## Section A - should for advice and opinions

**should** = it is a good thing to do or the right thing to do:
- You look tired. You **should go** to bed.
- The government **should do** more to improve schools.
- **Should** we **invite** Stephanie to the party? - Yes, I think we **should**.
- The man on the motorbike **should be wearing** a helmet.

**shouldn't** = it isn't a good thing to do:
- You **shouldn't believe** everything you read in newspapers.

### Often used with I think / I don't think / Do you think:
- I think the government **should do** more to improve schools.
- I **don't think** you **should work** so hard.

### should vs must / have to (strength):
- You **should** apologise. (= it would be a good thing to do)
- You **must** apologise. / You **have to** apologise. (= you have no alternative)

## Section B - should for expectations

**should** = what we expect to happen:
- Where's Tina? She **should be** here by now. (= I expect her to be here)
- Helen has been studying hard, so she **should pass** the exam.
- There are plenty of hotels. It **shouldn't be** hard to find a place to stay.

## Section C - should have (done) for past regret/criticism

**should have done** = you didn't do it, but it would have been good:
- You missed a great party. You **should have come**. Why didn't you?
- I wonder why they're so late. They **should have been** here long ago.

**shouldn't have done** = you did it, but it wasn't a good thing:
- I'm feeling sick. I **shouldn't have eaten** so much.
- She **shouldn't have been listening** to our conversation. It was private.

### Compare should (do) and should have (done):
- You look tired. You **should go** to bed now. (present advice)
- You went to bed very late last night. You **should have gone** to bed earlier. (past regret)

## Section D - ought to

**ought to** = **should** (with **to**):
- Do you think I **ought to apply** for this job? (= should I apply?)
- Jack **ought not to go** to bed so late.
- It was a great party. You **ought to have come**.
