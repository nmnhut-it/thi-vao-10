# Unit 48 - Reported speech 2

## A: When you don't always need to change the verb

If the situation is **still the same**, it is not necessary to change the verb to the past:
- *direct:* Paul said, 'My new job **is** boring.'
- *reported:* Paul said that his new job **is** boring. *(situation is still the same)*
- *direct:* Helen said, 'I **want** to go to Canada next year.'
- *reported:* Helen told me that she **wants** to go to Canada next year. *(Helen still wants to go)*

You **can also** change the verb to the past:
- Paul said that his new job **was** boring.
- Helen told me that she **wanted** to go to Canada next year.

**But** if the situation has changed or finished, you **need** to use a past verb:
- Paul left the room suddenly. He said (that) **he had to go.** *(not has to go)*

## B: Using the past when what was said is different from reality

- Rachel said: 'Have you heard? **Joe is in hospital.**'
- Later you meet Joe in the street. You say: 'Rachel said you **were** in hospital.' *(not 'are in hospital' -- it's clear he isn't)*

## C: say and tell

Use **tell** when you say *who* somebody is talking to:
- Rachel **told me** that you were in hospital. *(not Rachel said me)*
- What did you **tell the police**? *(not say the police)*

Otherwise use **say**:
- Rachel **said** that you were in hospital. *(not Rachel told that...)*
- What did you **say**?

You can 'say something **to** somebody':
- Anna **said** goodbye **to** me and left. *(not Anna said me goodbye)*

## D: tell somebody to ... / ask somebody to ...

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| 'Drink plenty of water,' the doctor said to me. | The doctor **told me to drink** plenty of water. |
| 'Don't work too hard,' I said to Joe. | I **told Joe not to work** too hard. |
| 'Can you help me, please,' Jackie said to me. | Jackie **asked me to help** her. |

You can also say 'Somebody **said (not) to** do something':
- Paul **said not to worry** about him.
