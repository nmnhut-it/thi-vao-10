# Unit 30 - Exercises: may and might 2

## Exercise 30.1 - Which alternative makes sense?
Choose between might (uncertain) and going to (certain).

1. I haven't decided yet. **I might go** to Ireland. (*not* I'm going)
2. Have you decided what car to buy? - Yes, **I'm going to get** a sports car.
3. When is Tom coming? - **He might come** on Sunday. (uncertain)
4. Where are you going to put that picture? - **I'm going to hang** it in the bedroom. (decided)
5. What's Tanya going to do? - **She's going** to university. (decided)
6. Do you have plans for the weekend? - **I might go away**. (uncertain)

## Exercise 30.2 - Complete using might + verb from box
Verbs: hear, need, rain, slip, spill, wake

1. Take an umbrella. It **might rain** later.
2-6. Complete sentences with might + appropriate verb.

## Exercise 30.3 - Complete using might be able to or might have to + verb
Verbs: fix, help, leave, meet, pay, wait

1. Tell me about your problem. I **might be able to help** you.
2-6. Distinguish between possibility of ability and possibility of obligation.

## Exercise 30.4 - Write sentences with might not
Express uncertainty about future events using might not.

1. Lisa's not feeling well. **Lisa might not come to the party.**
2-5. Write might not sentences from situational descriptions.

## Exercise 30.5 - Write sentences with might as well
Use might as well when there's no better alternative.

1. You've missed the bus. **We might as well walk.**
2-4. Create might as well sentences from situations.
