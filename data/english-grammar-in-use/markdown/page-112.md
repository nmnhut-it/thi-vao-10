# Unit 50 - Questions 2 (do you know where ...? / he asked me where ...)

## A: Embedded questions (Do you know where ...? / I don't know why ...)

When the question is part of a longer sentence (**Do you know ...?** / **I don't know ...** / **Can you tell me ...?**), the word order changes — **no inversion**:

| Direct question | Embedded question |
|---|---|
| What time **is it**? | Do you know what time **it is**? |
| Who **are** those people? | I don't know who those people **are**. |
| Where **can** I find Louise? | **Can** you tell me where I **can** find Louise? |
| How much **will** it cost? | Do you have any idea how much it **will** cost? |

### Be careful with do/does/did questions:
- What time **does** the film **start**? → Do you know what time **the film starts**? *(not does the film start)*
- What **do** you mean? → Please explain what **you mean**.
- Why **did** she leave early? → I wonder why **she left** early.

### Use **if** or **whether** when there is no other question word:
- Did anybody see you? → I don't know **if/whether** anybody saw me.

## B: Reported questions (He asked me where ...)

The same word order changes happen in reported speech:
- *direct:* 'Where **are you going**?' → The police officer asked us where **we were going**.
- *direct:* 'What time **do the shops close**?' → Clare wanted to know what time **the shops closed**.

In reported speech the verb usually changes to the past (were, closed etc.). See Unit 47.

### Job interview example (direct → reported):
- 'Are you willing to travel?' → She asked if (or whether) I **was** willing to travel.
- 'What do you do in your spare time?' → She wanted to know what I **did** in my spare time.
- 'How long have you been working in your present job?' → She asked how long I **had been working** in my present job.
- 'Why did you apply for the job?' → She asked why I **had applied** for the job.
- 'Can you speak any other languages?' → She wanted to know if (or whether) I **could** speak any other languages.
- 'Do you have a driving licence?' → She asked if (or whether) I **had** a driving licence.
