# Conditionals and Wish - Cau dieu kien va Wish

> Reference for Vietnamese Grade 9 students preparing for the entrance exam to Grade 10.

---

## 1. Type 0 - Conditional of General Truth (Dieu kien chung, su that hien nhien)

### Formation

> **If + S + V (present simple), S + V (present simple)**

### When to use

Describe facts, scientific truths, or things that always happen as a result of a condition. Both clauses use present simple.

### Examples

- If you heat water to 100 degrees Celsius, it **boils**. (Neu ban dun nuoc den 100 do, nuoc soi.)
- If it rains, the ground **gets** wet. (Neu troi mua, mat dat bi uot.)
- Plants die if they **don't** get enough sunlight. (Cay chet neu khong co du anh sang.)

> **Note:** You can replace "if" with "when" with no change in meaning for Type 0:
> - **When** you mix red and blue, you get purple.

---

## 2. Type 1 - Real Conditional (Dieu kien co that o hien tai/tuong lai)

### Formation

> **If + S + V (present simple), S + will/can/may + V (bare infinitive)**

### When to use

Talk about situations that are **possible or likely** in the present or future. The condition is real -- it may or may not happen.

### Examples

- If it **rains** tomorrow, we **will stay** at home. (Neu ngay mai troi mua, chung toi se o nha.)
- If you **study** hard, you **will pass** the exam. (Neu ban hoc cham chi, ban se thi dau.)
- If she **arrives** early, we **can** go to the cinema. (Neu co ay den som, chung ta co the di xem phim.)

> **Exam trap:** Do NOT use "will" in the if-clause for Type 1.
> - If it **rains** ... (NOT If it will rain)
> - But you CAN use "will" in the if-clause for requests: If you **will** wait a moment, I'll find someone to help you.

### Transformation patterns

- Study hard **or** you will fail. --> If you **don't** study hard, you **will** fail.
- Be careful **or** you will fall. --> If you **aren't** careful, you **will** fall.
- Come early **and** I will help you. --> If you **come** early, I **will** help you.

---

## 3. Type 2 - Unreal Conditional (Dieu kien khong co that o hien tai)

### Formation

> **If + S + V (past simple), S + would/could/might + V (bare infinitive)**

Special: Use **were** (not was) for all persons after "if" in formal English:
- If I **were** you, I would apologise. (Neu toi la ban, toi se xin loi.)

### When to use

Imagine situations that are **not true now** or are **unlikely to happen**. The past tense does NOT refer to the past -- it signals unreality.

### Examples

- If I **had** a million dollars, I **would travel** around the world. (Neu toi co mot trieu do, toi se di du lich vong quanh the gioi.) -- but I don't have it
- If she **knew** the answer, she **would tell** us. (Neu co ay biet cau tra loi, co ay se noi cho chung toi.) -- but she doesn't know
- If I **were** the president, I **would** build more schools. (Neu toi la tong thong, toi se xay them truong hoc.)

> **Exam trap:** Do NOT use "would" in the if-clause.
> - If I **knew** ... (NOT If I would know)
> - Could and might are also possible in the main clause:
>   - If it stopped raining, we **could** go out.
>   - If I won the lottery, I **might** buy a house.

### Transformation patterns

- I don't have a car, so I can't drive to work. --> If I **had** a car, I **could** drive to work.
- She is busy, so she can't come. --> If she **weren't** busy, she **could** come.
- He doesn't study, so he fails. --> If he **studied**, he **wouldn't** fail.

---

## 4. Type 3 - Past Unreal Conditional (Dieu kien khong co that trong qua khu)

### Formation

> **If + S + had + V3/ed (past perfect), S + would/could/might + have + V3/ed**

### When to use

Imagine how things **would have been different** if something in the past had been different. The condition was NOT fulfilled -- you are looking back with regret or speculation.

### Examples

- If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed** the exam. (Neu toi hoc cham chi hon, toi da thi dau.) -- but I didn't study hard, so I failed
- If she **had taken** a taxi, she **wouldn't have been** late. (Neu co ay bat taxi, co ay da khong den tre.)
- If we **had known** about the traffic, we **could have left** earlier. (Neu chung toi biet ve ket xe, chung toi da co the di som hon.)

> **Exam trap:** Do NOT use "would have" in the if-clause.
> - If I **had known** ... (NOT If I would have known)

### Transformation patterns

- I didn't wake up early, so I missed the bus. --> If I **had woken** up early, I **wouldn't have missed** the bus.
- She didn't tell me, so I didn't know. --> If she **had told** me, I **would have known**.
- He was careless, so he had an accident. --> If he **hadn't been** careless, he **wouldn't have had** an accident.
- Because he didn't have money, he couldn't buy the book. --> If he **had had** money, he **could have bought** the book.

---

## 5. Mixed Conditionals (Cau dieu kien hon hop)

### Type 3 condition + Type 2 result (qua khu --> hien tai)

Past action affects present situation:

> **If + S + had + V3/ed, S + would + V (bare infinitive)**

- If I **had accepted** that job, I **would be** rich now. (Neu toi da nhan cong viec do, bay gio toi se giau.)
- If she **hadn't missed** the flight, she **would be** in Paris now. (Neu co ay khong lo chuyen bay, bay gio co ay se o Paris.)

### Type 2 condition + Type 3 result (hien tai --> qua khu)

Present state affected past action:

> **If + S + V (past simple), S + would have + V3/ed**

- If I **were** taller, I **would have joined** the basketball team last year. (Neu toi cao hon, nam ngoai toi da gia nhap doi bong ro.)
- If he **spoke** English, he **would have got** that job. (Neu anh ay biet tieng Anh, anh ay da duoc nhan viec do.)

## 6. UNLESS (Tru khi, neu khong)

### Meaning

**Unless** = **if ... not**. It introduces the only condition that would prevent something.

### Examples

- **Unless** you hurry, you will be late. = If you **don't** hurry, you will be late. (Tru khi ban nhanh len, ban se tre.)
- I won't go **unless** she invites me. = I won't go **if** she **doesn't** invite me. (Toi se khong di tru khi co ay moi toi.)
- **Unless** it rains, we will have the picnic. (Tru khi troi mua, chung toi se di da ngoai.)

> **Exam trap:** Do not use "unless" with negative verbs -- it creates a double negative.
> - Unless you study ... (NOT Unless you don't study)

### Transformation

- If you don't leave now, you'll miss the train. --> **Unless** you leave now, you'll miss the train.

---

## 7. IN CASE (Phong khi, de phong)

### Meaning

**In case** = because something might happen. You do something **before** the situation arises, as a precaution.

### Examples

- Take an umbrella **in case** it **rains**. (Mang du de phong troi mua.) -- I take the umbrella NOW because it MIGHT rain later.
- I'll give you my number **in case** you **need** to contact me. (Toi se cho ban so dien thoai phong khi ban can lien lac.)

> **Key distinction IN CASE vs IF:**
> - I'll take an umbrella **in case** it rains. (= I'll take it anyway, as a precaution)
> - I'll take an umbrella **if** it rains. (= I'll only take it when it actually rains)

---

## 8. PROVIDED / AS LONG AS (Mien la, voi dieu kien la)

### Meaning

**Provided (that)** and **as long as** mean "only if" -- they set a strict condition.

### Examples

- You can borrow my car **provided (that)** you drive carefully. (Ban co the muon xe toi mien la ban lai can than.)
- I'll help you **as long as** you promise to work hard. (Toi se giup ban voi dieu kien ban hua se lam viec cham chi.)
- We'll go to the beach **provided** the weather is good. (Chung toi se di bien mien la thoi tiet tot.)

> **Note:** "Providing (that)" has the same meaning as "provided (that)".

---

## 9. WISH / IF ONLY (Uoc gi)

### Wish about the present (uoc ve hien tai)

> **S + wish(es) + S + V (past simple/past subjunctive)**

Use past tense to express a wish about something that is NOT true now:
- I **wish** I **knew** the answer. (Uoc gi toi biet cau tra loi.) -- but I don't know
- She **wishes** she **were** taller. (Co ay uoc co ay cao hon.) -- but she isn't
- I **wish** I **didn't have** to work tomorrow. (Uoc gi ngay mai toi khong phai lam viec.) -- but I do

**If only** is stronger and more emotional than **wish**:
- **If only** I **had** more time! (Gia ma toi co nhieu thoi gian hon!)
- **If only** she **were** here! (Gia ma co ay o day!)

### Wish about the past (uoc ve qua khu - hoi tiec)

> **S + wish(es) + S + had + V3/ed (past perfect)**

Express regret about something that happened or didn't happen:
- I **wish** I **had studied** harder. (Uoc gi toi da hoc cham chi hon.) -- but I didn't
- She **wishes** she **hadn't said** that. (Co ay uoc co ay da khong noi dieu do.) -- but she said it
- **If only** we **had taken** your advice! (Gia ma chung toi da nghe loi khuyen cua ban!)

### Wish about the future (muon ai lam gi / muon dieu gi xay ra)

> **S + wish(es) + S + would + V (bare infinitive)**

Express a desire for something to change, or annoyance about a present habit:
- I **wish** it **would stop** raining. (Toi uoc troi ngung mua.) -- complaining
- I **wish** you **would** be quiet. (Toi muon ban im lang.) -- annoyance
- She **wishes** he **would** call her. (Co ay muon anh ay goi cho co ay.)

> **Exam trap:** Do NOT use "wish ... would" about yourself or about permanent states.
> - I wish I **knew** the answer. (NOT I wish I would know)
> - I wish I **were** taller. (NOT I wish I would be taller)
> - Use "wish ... would" only when you want someone/something ELSE to change.

### Transformation patterns for Wish

- I'm sorry I don't speak French. --> I **wish** I **spoke** French.
- It's a pity she isn't here. --> I **wish** she **were** here.
- I regret not studying harder. --> I **wish** I **had studied** harder.
- I'm sorry I said that. --> I **wish** I **hadn't said** that.
- He is always late. (annoying) --> I **wish** he **wouldn't** be late all the time.

## 10. IT'S TIME (Da den luc)

### Formation

> **It's time + S + V (past simple)**

The past tense here does NOT mean past time. It means "this should happen now (or should have happened already)".

### Examples

- It's late. **It's time** we **went** home. (Da muon roi. Da den luc chung ta ve nha.)
- **It's time** the children **went** to bed. (Da den luc bon tre di ngu.)
- This situation can't continue. **It's time** you **did** something about it. (Tinh hinh nay khong the tiep tuc. Da den luc ban lam gi do.)

**It's about time** and **It's high time** are stronger -- they criticize that something is overdue:
- He's very selfish. **It's about time** he **thought** about other people. (Anh ay rat ich ky. Da qua den luc anh ay nghi cho nguoi khac.)
- **It's high time** the government **took** action on pollution. (Da qua den luc chinh phu hanh dong ve o nhiem.)

> **Note:** You can also say "It's time **to do** something" (simpler, no criticism):
> - It's time **to go**. = It's time we **went**.

---

## 11. WOULD RATHER + clause (Thich hon / Muon)

### Formation

> **S + would rather + (that) + S + V (past simple)** -- present/future meaning
> **S + would rather + (that) + S + had + V3/ed** -- past meaning

### Examples (present/future)

- I**'d rather** you **came** tomorrow, not today. (Toi muon ban den ngay mai, khong phai hom nay.)
- She**'d rather** we **didn't** mention this to anyone. (Co ay muon chung toi khong noi dieu nay voi ai.)
- **Would** you **rather** I **drove**? (Ban co muon toi lai xe khong?)

### Examples (past)

- I**'d rather** you **had told** me the truth. (Toi uoc ban da noi su that cho toi.)

> **Key point:** This follows the same "unreal past" pattern as wish and Type 2 conditionals. Past form = present/future meaning.

|------|-----------|-------------|------|---------|
| 0 | present simple | present simple | general | If you heat ice, it melts. |
| 1 | present simple | will + V | future (real) | If it rains, I will stay home. |

## Common Exam Rewrite Patterns

**1. Type 2 from reality:**
- I don't have a car. I can't drive to work. --> If I **had** a car, I **could** drive to work.

**2. Type 3 from past:**
- He didn't study. He failed. --> If he **had studied**, he **wouldn't have failed**.

**3. Wish from reality:**
- I can't swim. --> I **wish** I **could** swim.
- She left early. --> She **wishes** she **hadn't left** early.

**4. Unless from if...not:**
- If you don't hurry, you'll be late. --> **Unless** you hurry, you'll be late.

**5. It's time from should:**
- You should start studying now. --> **It's time** you **started** studying.

**6. Would rather from preference:**
- I prefer you to go by train. --> I**'d rather** you **went** by train.

**7. Provided/As long as from condition:**
- You can go out if you finish your homework. --> You can go out **as long as** you finish your homework.
