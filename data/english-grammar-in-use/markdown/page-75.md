# Unit 31 - Exercises: have to and must

## Exercise 31.1 - Complete with have/has/had to
Use verbs in brackets.

1. Robert can't come out this evening. **He has to work** late. (he / work)
2. 'How long **did you have to wait**?' (you / wait)
3-11. Complete sentences using have to in various tenses.

## Exercise 31.2 - Complete with have/has/had to + verb (some negative)
Verbs: ask, decide, drive, get up, go, make, make, pay, show, stand

1. I'm not working tomorrow, so I **don't have to get up** early.
2. Steve didn't know how to change the settings on his phone. I **had to show** him.
3-10. Complete sentences, some requiring negative forms (don't have to).

## Exercise 31.3 - Is must wrong or unnatural? Correct where necessary.
Replace **must** with **have to** where must sounds wrong or unnatural.

1. It's later than I thought. I must go. - **OK (I have to go is also correct)**
2. I must start work every day at 8.30. - **I have to start work** (fact, not opinion)
3-8. Continue correcting unnatural uses of must.

## Exercise 31.4 - Complete with mustn't, don't have to or doesn't have to
Distinguish prohibition from lack of necessity.

1. I don't want anyone to know about our plan. You **mustn't** tell anyone.
2. Richard **doesn't have to** wear a suit to work, but he usually does.
3-10. Continue distinguishing mustn't vs don't have to.
