# Unit 35 - I'd better ... it's time ...

## Section A - had better (I'd better / you'd better etc.)

**I'd better do** something = it is advisable to do it; if I don't, there will be a problem or danger:
- I have to meet Amy in ten minutes. **I'd better go** now or I'll be late.
- 'Shall I take an umbrella?' 'Yes, **you'd better**. It might rain.'
- **We'd better stop** for petrol soon. The tank is almost empty.

### Negative: I'd better not
- That jacket looks good on you. Are you going to buy it? - **I'd better not**. It's very expensive.
- You don't look very well. **You'd better not go** out tonight.

### Key points:
- I'd better = I **had** better (not "I would better")
- **Had** is normally past, but **had better** is for present or future (*not* past)
- We say 'I'd better **do**' (*not* to do)

## Section B - had better vs should

- **Had better** = specific situation only, implies danger/problem if not followed
- **Should** = general advice, all types of situations, no implied danger

**Compare:**
- It's late. You'**d better go**. *or* You **should go**. (specific situation)
- You're always at home. You **should go** out more often. (general - *not* 'had better go')

Also with **had better**, there is always a danger or problem:
- It's a great film. You **should** go and see it. (but no problem if you don't)
- The film starts at 8.30. You'**d better** go now or you'll miss the beginning. (danger of missing it)

## Section C - it's time ...

**It's time (for somebody) to do ...**:
- It's time **to go** home. / It's time for us **to go** home.

**It's time + past** (meaning is present, not past):
- It's late. It's time **we went** home. (*not* It's time we go)
- It's time they **were** here. Why are they so late?

**It's time somebody did something** = they should have already done it or started it (often used for criticism):
- This situation can't continue. **It's time** you **did** something about it.
- He's very selfish. **It's time he realised** that he isn't the most important person.

You can also say **It's about time ...**:
- Jack is a great talker, but **it's about time he did** something instead of just talking.
