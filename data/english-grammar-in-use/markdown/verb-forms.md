# Verb Forms: Gerund (V-ing) vs Infinitive (to V)

## Dang dong tu: V-ing va to V (Cac hinh thuc dong tu)

---

## 1. Verbs Followed by V-ing (Dong tu theo sau boi V-ing)

Some verbs must be followed by **-ing**, not **to ...**.

### Common verbs + V-ing

| Verb | Vietnamese | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| enjoy | thuong thuc | I **enjoy reading** novels. |
| mind | phien, ngai | Would you **mind closing** the door? |
| suggest | goi y | She **suggested going** to the beach. |
| stop | ngung | He **stopped smoking** last year. |
| finish | hoan thanh | Have you **finished doing** your homework? |
| avoid | tranh | Try to **avoid making** mistakes. |
| consider | can nhac | Have you **considered studying** abroad? |
| admit | thua nhan | He **admitted stealing** the money. |
| deny | phu nhan | She **denied breaking** the vase. |
| imagine | tuong tuong | Can you **imagine living** without a phone? |
| risk | mao hiem | You **risk losing** everything. |
| keep (on) | tiep tuc | She **keeps interrupting** me. |
| give up | tu bo | He has **given up eating** fast food. |
| put off | hoan lai | Don't **put off doing** your assignment. |
| go on | tiep tuc | They **went on talking** for hours. |
| fancy | thich, muon | I don't **fancy going** out tonight. |

**Key rule (Quy tac quan trong):** After **enjoy**, **mind**, and **suggest**, always use **-ing**, never **to ...**.

- I enjoy **reading**. *(not: I enjoy to read)*
- She suggested **meeting** at 6. *(not: She suggested to meet at 6)*

---

## 2. Verbs Followed by to V (Dong tu theo sau boi to V)

Some verbs must be followed by **to + infinitive**.

### Common verbs + to V

| Verb | Vietnamese | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| decide | quyet dinh | We **decided to leave** early. |
| want | muon | I **want to learn** English. |
| hope | hy vong | She **hopes to pass** the exam. |
| plan | len ke hoach | They **plan to visit** Ha Long Bay. |
| agree | dong y | He **agreed to help** us. |
| offer | de nghi | She **offered to carry** my bag. |
| refuse | tu choi | He **refused to answer** the question. |
| promise | hua | I **promised to be** on time. |
| forget | quen | Don't **forget to lock** the door. |
| learn | hoc | She is **learning to drive**. |
| manage | xoay xo | We **managed to arrive** on time. |
| afford | co du kha nang | I can't **afford to buy** a new phone. |
| fail | that bai | He **failed to pass** the test. |
| tend | co xu huong | It **tends to rain** in the afternoon. |
| arrange | sap xep | We **arranged to meet** at the cafe. |
| threaten | de doa | She **threatened to call** the police. |
| seem | co ve | They **seem to have** a lot of money. |
| pretend | gia vo | He **pretended to be** asleep. |
| appear | co ve nhu | She **appears to know** everybody. |
| dare | dam | I didn't **dare to tell** him the truth. |

**Negative form (The phu dinh):** We **decided not to go** out. *(not: decided to not go)*

## 3. Verbs That Take Both V-ing and to V with Different Meanings

These verbs are especially important for the Grade 9 exam (Thi vao 10). The meaning changes depending on whether you use **-ing** or **to ...**.

### 3.1 REMEMBER (nho)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| remember + V-ing | nho da lam viec gi (in the past) | Nho lai viec da xay ra | I **remember locking** the door. (= I locked it, and I remember this now.) |
| remember + to V | nho phai lam viec gi (for the future) | Nho se lam viec gi do | **Remember to lock** the door. (= Don't forget to lock it.) |

### 3.2 FORGET (quen)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| forget + V-ing | quen da lam viec gi | Quen rang da lam roi | I'll never **forget visiting** Paris. (= I visited Paris and will always remember it.) |
| forget + to V | quen phai lam viec gi | Quen khong lam | She **forgot to buy** milk. (= She didn't buy it because she forgot.) |

### 3.3 STOP (ngung, dung lai)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| stop + V-ing | ngung lam viec gi | Dung han lam | He **stopped smoking**. (= He no longer smokes.) |
| stop + to V | dung lai de lam viec gi | Dung lai nham lam | He **stopped to smoke**. (= He paused what he was doing in order to have a cigarette.) |

### 3.4 TRY (thu, co gang)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| try + V-ing | thu lam | Thu xem sao | **Try pressing** the green button. (= Do it as an experiment to see what happens.) |
| try + to V | co gang lam | No luc lam | I **tried to open** the window, but it was stuck. (= I attempted but could not do it.) |

### 3.5 REGRET (hoi tiec)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| regret + V-ing | hoi tiec da lam | Hoi han ve viec da lam | I **regret saying** that. (= I said it and now I'm sorry.) |
| regret + to V | lay lam tiec phai noi | Tiec phai thong bao | I **regret to inform** you that you failed. (= I'm sorry to tell you this news.) |

### 3.6 GO ON (tiep tuc)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| go on + V-ing | tiep tuc lam cung viec | Tiep tuc viec dang lam | She **went on talking** for an hour. (= She continued talking about the same thing.) |
| go on + to V | chuyen sang lam viec khac | Tiep tuc lam viec moi | After the introduction, she **went on to explain** the details. (= She moved to a new topic.) |

### 3.7 MEAN (co nghia, dinh)

| Structure | Meaning | Vietnamese | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| mean + V-ing | co nghia la | Dong nghia voi, dan den | Passing the exam **means studying** hard. (= It involves studying hard.) |
| mean + to V | co y dinh | Dinh lam | I didn't **mean to hurt** you. (= I didn't intend to hurt you.) |

## 4. Verb + Object + to V (Dong tu + Tan ngu + to V)

Some verbs follow the pattern: **verb + somebody + to do something**.

### Common verbs in this pattern

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| want | I **want you to be** careful. |
| ask | She **asked me to help** her. |
| tell | The teacher **told us to sit** down. |
| expect | We **expected them to arrive** at 8. |
| advise | I **advise you to study** harder. |
| invite | They **invited us to come** to the party. |
| remind | Can you **remind me to call** her? |
| warn | She **warned him not to touch** it. |
| encourage | My parents **encouraged me to study** English. |
| persuade | He **persuaded her to change** her mind. |
| allow | They don't **allow students to use** phones. |
| force | Nobody **forced you to do** it. |
| teach | Who **taught you to cook**? |
| get | I **got my brother to help** me. |

**Important (Luu y):** Do not use **suggest** with this pattern.
- Jane **suggested that I ask** you for advice. *(not: Jane suggested me to ask)*

## 5. Make / Let / Help + Bare Infinitive (Dong tu nguyen the khong "to")

Three special verbs are followed by **object + verb** (without **to**). This is called the **bare infinitive** (dong tu nguyen the tran).

### 5.1 MAKE (buc, khien)

**make + somebody + V (bare infinitive)**

- Hot weather **makes me feel** tired. *(Thoi tiet nong khien toi cam thay met.)*
- The movie **made her cry**. *(Bo phim khien co ay khoc.)*
- My parents **made me clean** my room. *(Bo me bat toi don phong.)*

**In the passive (The bi dong):** use **made + to V**
- I **was made to clean** my room. *(not: was made clean)*
- We **were made to wait** for two hours.

### 5.2 LET (cho phep, de cho)

**let + somebody + V (bare infinitive)**

- Her parents **let her go** out. *(Bo me cho co ay di choi.)*
- **Let me carry** your bag. *(De toi xach tui cho ban.)*
- Don't **let him know** about the surprise. *(Dung de anh ay biet ve bat ngo.)*

**Note (Luu y):** We do not use **let** in the passive. Use **be allowed to** instead.
- She **was allowed to go** out. *(not: She was let go out.)*

### 5.3 HELP (giup)

**help + somebody + V (bare infinitive)** OR **help + somebody + to V**

Both forms are correct with **help**:
- Can you **help me move** this table? *(bare infinitive)*
- Can you **help me to move** this table? *(to-infinitive)*
- Everybody **helped clean** up after the party.
- Everybody **helped to clean** up after the party.

## 6. Summary Table for Quick Review (Bang tom tat)

| Pattern | Verbs | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| V + V-ing | enjoy, mind, suggest, stop, finish, avoid, admit, deny, imagine, risk, keep, give up | I enjoy **swimming**. |
| V + to V | decide, want, hope, plan, agree, offer, refuse, promise, forget, learn, manage, afford | She decided **to leave**. |
| V + V-ing OR to V (same meaning) | begin, start, continue, intend, bother | It started **raining** / **to rain**. |
| V + V-ing OR to V (different meaning) | remember, forget, stop, try, regret, go on, mean | Remember **to lock** / **locking** |
| V + object + to V | want, ask, tell, advise, invite, remind, warn, encourage, persuade, allow | I asked him **to wait**. |
| make/let + object + bare V | make, let | She made me **laugh**. |
| help + object + (to) V | help | Help me **(to) carry** this. |

## 7. Common Exam Mistakes (Loi thuong gap trong bai thi)

1. **Wrong:** I enjoy *to read* books. **Right:** I enjoy **reading** books.
2. **Wrong:** She suggested *me to go*. **Right:** She suggested **going** / suggested **that I go**.
3. **Wrong:** He made me *to do* it. **Right:** He made me **do** it.
4. **Wrong:** I was made *do* it. **Right:** I was made **to do** it.
5. **Wrong:** Remember *buying* milk on your way home. **Right:** Remember **to buy** milk.
6. **Wrong:** I stopped *to smoke* last year. **Right:** I **stopped smoking** last year. *(if you mean you quit)*
7. **Wrong:** He let me *to use* his car. **Right:** He let me **use** his car.

---

## 8. Practice Tips (Meo luyen tap)

- When you see **enjoy, mind, suggest, avoid, finish** in a sentence, the next verb must end in **-ing**.
- When you see **decide, want, hope, plan, agree, refuse, promise**, the next verb must be **to + V**.
- For **remember, forget, stop, try, regret, go on, mean**, think about whether the action is in the past (use -ing) or in the future/intended (use to V).
- For **make** and **let**, drop the **to**. For passive with **make**, add **to** back.
