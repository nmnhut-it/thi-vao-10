# Subject-Verb Concord (Su hoa hop giua chu ngu va dong tu)

## Su hoa hop chu ngu - vi ngu

---

## 1. Basic Principle (Nguyen tac co ban)

In English, the verb must **agree** with its subject in number:
- **Singular subject** -> **singular verb** (dong tu so it)
- **Plural subject** -> **plural verb** (dong tu so nhieu)

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| He (singular) | **works** | He works hard. |
| They (plural) | **work** | They work hard. |
| The dog (singular) | **is** | The dog is friendly. |
| The dogs (plural) | **are** | The dogs are friendly. |

---

## 2. Collective Nouns (Danh tu tap hop)

Collective nouns (danh tu tap hop) refer to a group of people or things: **family, team, class, audience, government, committee, staff, crew, group, crowd, police**.

### Rule: Can be singular OR plural depending on meaning

| Meaning | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| The group as **one unit** (mot don vi) | Singular | My family **is** small. *(Gia dinh toi nho.)* |
| The **individual members** (cac thanh vien) | Plural | My family **are** all teachers. *(Cac thanh vien deu la giao vien.)* |

More examples:
- The team **is** the best in the city. *(the team as one unit)*
- The team **are** wearing their new shirts. *(each member wearing their own shirt)*
- The audience **was** large. *(one group)*
- The audience **were** clapping their hands. *(individuals doing their own action)*
- The class **has** 40 students. *(one class)*
- The class **have** handed in their essays. *(individual students)*

### Special case: POLICE is always plural

- The police **are** looking for the thief. *(not: The police is)*
- The police **have** arrested two men.

### Special case: NEWS, MATHEMATICS, PHYSICS are always singular

- The **news is** bad today. *(not: The news are)*
- **Mathematics is** my favourite subject.
- **Physics was** difficult for me.

---

## 3. Each / Every / Everyone / Everybody + Singular Verb

Words beginning with **each, every, everyone, everybody, everything, someone, somebody, something, anyone, anybody, anything, no one, nobody, nothing** always take a **singular verb**.

| Subject | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Each | **Each** student **has** a textbook. |
| Every | **Every** child **needs** love. |
| Everyone | **Everyone is** here. |
| Everybody | **Everybody was** tired. |
| Everything | **Everything is** ready. |
| Someone | **Someone has** left a bag. |
| Nobody | **Nobody knows** the answer. |
| Nothing | **Nothing was** stolen. |

**Important (Luu y):**
- **Each of** the students **has** a book. *(singular)*
- **Every one of** the rooms **is** clean. *(singular)*
- **Everyone** in the class **has** passed. *(singular)*

---

## 4. Either / Neither + Singular Verb

When **either** or **neither** is used alone as a subject, the verb is **singular**.

| Subject | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Either | **Either** is fine with me. *(Ca hai deu duoc.)* |
| Neither | **Neither** was suitable. *(Ca hai deu khong phu hop.)* |
| Either of | **Either of** the answers **is** correct. |
| Neither of | **Neither of** them **knows** the truth. |

**Note:** In informal English, you may hear "Neither of them **know**..." but the formal/exam rule is **singular**.

---

## 5. Either...or / Neither...nor: Nearest Subject Rule (Quy tac chu ngu gan nhat)

When **either...or** or **neither...nor** connects two subjects, the verb agrees with the **subject nearest to it** (chu ngu gan dong tu nhat).

| Sentence | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| Either you or **he is** wrong. | "he" is nearest -> singular |
| Either he or **you are** wrong. | "you" is nearest -> plural |
| Neither the teacher nor **the students were** ready. | "students" is nearest -> plural |
| Neither the students nor **the teacher was** ready. | "teacher" is nearest -> singular |
| Either my brothers or **my father is** going to help. | "father" is nearest -> singular |
| Either my father or **my brothers are** going to help. | "brothers" is nearest -> plural |

**Tip (Meo):** In exams, when you see either...or or neither...nor, look at the subject **closest to the verb** to decide singular or plural.

---

## 6. A Number of vs The Number of

This pair is extremely common in exams (Rat hay gap trong bai thi).

| Expression | Verb | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|---------|
| **A number of** + plural noun | **Plural** | nhieu (= many/several) | **A number of** students **were** absent. |
| **The number of** + plural noun | **Singular** | so luong (the quantity) | **The number of** students **is** increasing. |

More examples:
- **A number of** problems **have** been found. *(Nhieu van de da duoc tim thay.)*
- **The number of** problems **has** decreased. *(So luong van de da giam.)*
- **A number of** people **are** waiting. *(Nhieu nguoi dang doi.)*
- **The number of** people **is** growing. *(So luong nguoi dang tang.)*

**Memory trick (Meo ghi nho):**
- "**A** number of" = **A**lot of -> plural
- "**The** number of" = **The** quantity -> singular

---

## 7. There is / There are

The verb agrees with the **noun that follows** (chu ngu thuc su di sau).

| Sentence | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| **There is** a book on the table. | "a book" = singular |
| **There are** many books on the table. | "many books" = plural |
| **There was** an accident yesterday. | "an accident" = singular |
| **There were** two accidents yesterday. | "two accidents" = plural |
| **There has been** a change in plans. | "a change" = singular |
| **There have been** many changes. | "many changes" = plural |

**With a list of items:** The verb agrees with the **first noun**:
- There **is** a pen, two books, and a ruler on the desk. *(first item = "a pen" = singular)*
- There **are** two books, a pen, and a ruler on the desk. *(first item = "two books" = plural)*

---

## 8. Noun Phrases with "of" (Cum danh tu voi "of")

When the subject is a **quantity expression + of + noun**, the verb usually agrees with the **main noun before "of"**.

### Singular (the container/unit is the subject)

| Subject | Example |
|---------|---------|
| A box of chocolates | A box of chocolates **is** on the table. |
| A pair of shoes | A pair of shoes **costs** $50. |
| A piece of news | A piece of news **was** announced. |
| A cup of coffee | A cup of coffee **is** what I need. |
| A group of students | A group of students **is** studying. *(as one unit)* |
| A pack of wolves | A pack of wolves **was** seen. |

### Depends on the noun after "of"

Some expressions can go either way depending on meaning:

| Expression + of | Verb agrees with... | Example |
|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| **All of** the water | water (uncountable) = singular | All of the water **is** gone. |
| **All of** the students | students (plural) = plural | All of the students **are** here. |
| **Some of** the money | money (uncountable) = singular | Some of the money **was** stolen. |
| **Some of** the books | books (plural) = plural | Some of the books **are** missing. |
| **Most of** the work | work (uncountable) = singular | Most of the work **is** done. |
| **Most of** the people | people (plural) = plural | Most of the people **have** left. |
| **Half of** the cake | cake (singular) = singular | Half of the cake **was** eaten. |
| **Half of** the students | students (plural) = plural | Half of the students **were** late. |
| **None of** the money | singular preferred | None of the money **was** found. |
| **None of** the students | singular or plural | None of the students **has/have** passed. |

### Fractions and percentages

- **Two-thirds of** the land **is** forest.
- **Two-thirds of** the students **are** Vietnamese.
- **Fifty percent of** the work **has** been completed.
- **Fifty percent of** the workers **have** finished.

---

## 9. Both...and + Plural Verb

**Both...and** always takes a **plural** verb because it refers to two things together.

- **Both** Tom **and** Jerry **are** my friends.
- **Both** the teacher **and** the students **were** surprised.
- **Both** English **and** math **are** important subjects.
- **Both** my mother **and** my father **work** in a hospital.

---

## 10. Other Important Rules (Cac quy tac quan trong khac)

### 10.1 Uncountable nouns = Singular

Uncountable nouns (danh tu khong dem duoc) always take a singular verb:
- **Information is** important. *(not: Information are)*
- **Furniture was** expensive.
- **Advice is** what I need.
- **Money doesn't** grow on trees.
- **Knowledge is** power.

### 10.2 Plural-form subjects with singular meaning

Some nouns look plural (-s ending) but are singular:
- **The news is** shocking.
- **Mathematics is** difficult.
- **Physics was** my best subject.
- **The United States is** a large country.
- **Economics is** an interesting field.

### 10.3 Distances, amounts of money, periods of time = Singular

When we talk about a **total amount** as one unit:
- **Ten kilometers is** a long distance to walk.
- **Five thousand dollars is** a lot of money.
- **Three years is** a long time to wait.
- **Two hours is** enough time for the test.

### 10.4 Gerund / To-infinitive as subject = Singular

When a **gerund (V-ing)** or **to-infinitive** is the subject:
- **Swimming is** good exercise.
- **To learn** English **is** important.
- **Reading books makes** you smarter.

### 10.5 Titles of books, movies, etc. = Singular

- "**The Lord of the Rings" is** my favourite movie.
- "**Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" was** published in 1997.

---

## 11. Summary Table (Bang tom tat)

| Rule | Subject | Verb | Example |
|------|---------|------|---------|
| Each/Every/Everyone | singular | singular | Each student **has** a pen. |
| Either/Neither (alone) | singular | singular | Neither **is** correct. |
| Either...or / Neither...nor | nearest subject | agrees with nearest | Neither he nor they **are** ready. |
| Both...and | two items | plural | Both A and B **are** good. |
| A number of | = many | plural | A number of people **are** waiting. |
| The number of | = the quantity | singular | The number of people **is** rising. |
| There is/are | following noun | agrees with noun | There **are** two books. |
| Collective nouns | group | singular or plural | The team **is/are** ready. |
| Uncountable | - | singular | Water **is** essential. |
| Police | - | always plural | The police **are** here. |
| News/Math/Physics | - | always singular | The news **is** bad. |
| Amount as one unit | - | singular | Five dollars **is** enough. |

---

## 12. Common Exam Mistakes (Loi thuong gap trong bai thi)

1. **Wrong:** A number of students *is* absent. **Right:** A number of students **are** absent.
2. **Wrong:** The number of students *are* increasing. **Right:** The number of students **is** increasing.
3. **Wrong:** Everyone *are* here. **Right:** Everyone **is** here.
4. **Wrong:** Neither of them *know*. **Right:** Neither of them **knows**. *(formal)*
5. **Wrong:** The police *is* coming. **Right:** The police **are** coming.
6. **Wrong:** The news *are* bad. **Right:** The news **is** bad.
7. **Wrong:** Both Tom and Jerry *is* my friend. **Right:** Both Tom and Jerry **are** my friends.
8. **Wrong:** There *is* many people here. **Right:** There **are** many people here.
9. **Wrong:** Mathematics *are* easy. **Right:** Mathematics **is** easy.
10. **Wrong:** Neither the boys nor the girl *were* ready. **Right:** Neither the boys nor the girl **was** ready.
