# Unit 39 - if I knew ... I wish I knew ...

## Section A - Second Conditional with if I knew / if I had etc.

When we imagine a situation, we use **if + past** (if I knew / if you were / if we didn't etc.). The meaning is present, NOT past:
- **I'd phone** him **if** I **knew** his number. (I don't know his number)
- There are many things I'd like to do **if** I **had** more time.
- **If** I **didn't** want to go to the party, I wouldn't go.
- **If** you **were** in my position, what would you do?
- It's a pity he can't drive. It would be useful **if** he **could**.

## Section B - I wish I knew / I wish I had etc.

**wish** + past = we regret something, it is not as we would like it to be:
- I **wish** I **knew** Paul's phone number. (= I don't know it and I regret this)
- Do you ever **wish** you **could** fly?
- It's very crowded here. I **wish** there **weren't** so many people.
- I **wish** I **didn't** have to work tomorrow, but unfortunately I do.

### Compare:
- I'm glad I **live** here. (I live here and that's good)
- I **wish** I **lived** here. (I don't live here unfortunately)

## Section C - were instead of was after if and wish

After **if** and **wish**, you can use **were** instead of **was**:
- **If** I **were** you, I wouldn't buy that coat. *or* **If** I **was** you, ...
- I'd go for a walk **if** it **weren't** so cold. *or* ... **if** it **wasn't** so cold.
- I **wish** Anna **were** here. *or* I **wish** Anna **was** here.

## Section D - would in the result clause (not after if/wish)

We do NOT usually say 'if I would' or 'I wish I would':
- **If** I **were** rich, I **would** travel a lot. (*not* If I would be rich)
- I **wish** I **had** something to read. (*not* I wish I would have)
- I **wish** she **were** here now. (*not* I wish she would be)

## Section E - could after wish/if (= would be able to / was able to)

**could** sometimes means 'would be able to' or 'was/were able to':
- She **could get** a better job (= she **would be able to** get)
  if she **could speak** another language (= if she **was able to** speak).
- I wish I **could** help you. (= I wish I **was able** to help)
