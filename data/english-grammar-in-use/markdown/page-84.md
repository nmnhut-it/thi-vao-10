# Unit 36 - would

## Section A - would ('d) / wouldn't for imagined situations

**would** = when we imagine a situation or action that is not real:
- It **would be** nice to buy a new car, but we can't afford it.
- I'**d love** to live by the sea.
- Shall I tell Chris what happened? - No, I **wouldn't say** anything.

### would have (done) - imagining something that didn't happen in the past:
- They helped us a lot. I don't know what we'**d have done** without their help.
- It's a shame you didn't see the film. You **would have liked** it.
- I didn't tell Sam what happened. He **wouldn't have been** pleased.

### Compare would (do) and would have (done):
- I **would call** Lisa, but I don't have her number. (now)
- I **would have called** Lisa, but I didn't have her number. (past)

### Often used with if:
- I **would call** Lisa **if** I had her number.
- I **would have called** Lisa **if** I'd had her number.

## Section B - will ('ll) vs would ('d)

- I'**ll stay** a little longer. I've got plenty of time. (real - I will stay)
- I'**d stay** a little longer, but I really have to go now. (imagined - I can't stay)

### would/wouldn't as past of will/won't:
- TOM: I'**ll call** you on Sunday. -> Tom said he'**d call** me on Sunday.
- AMY: I promise I **won't be** late. -> Amy promised she **wouldn't be** late.

### wouldn't do = refused to do:
- I tried to warn him, but he **wouldn't listen** to me. (= he refused to listen)
- The car **wouldn't start**. (= it 'refused' to start)

## Section C - would for past habits

**would** = things that happened regularly in the past:
- When we were children, we lived by the sea. In summer, we **would** all get up early and go for a swim.
- Whenever Richard was angry, he **would** walk out of the room.

This meaning is similar to **used to** (see Unit 18).
