# Unit 32 - must, mustn't, needn't

## Section A - must and mustn't

- **must** = it is necessary that you do it:
  - Don't tell anybody what I said. You **must keep** it a secret.
  - We don't have much time. We **must hurry**.

- **mustn't** = don't do it:
  - You **must** keep it a secret. You **mustn't tell** anyone. (= don't tell anyone)
  - We **must** be very quiet. We **mustn't** make any noise.

## Section B - needn't and don't need to

**needn't** = it's not necessary to do it (but you can if you want):
- We have plenty of time. We **needn't hurry**.
- Joe can stay here. He **needn't come** with us.

You can also use **don't/doesn't need to**:
- We **don't need to** hurry.

**Note:** We say 'don't need **to** do', but 'needn't do' (without **to**).

### Compare needn't and mustn't:
- You **needn't** tell Steve. I can tell him myself. (= it is not necessary)
- You **mustn't** tell Steve. I don't want him to know. (= don't tell him)

## Section C - needn't have (done)

**needn't have done** = you did it, but now you know it was not necessary:
- They **needn't have reserved** a table. (They reserved one, but the restaurant was almost empty.)

### Compare:
- Everything will be OK. You **needn't worry**. (present - it is not necessary)
- Everything was OK. You **needn't have worried**. (past - you worried, but it was not necessary)

## Section D - needn't have vs didn't need to

- **needn't have done** = he did it, but it was not necessary: He **needn't have got** up so early.
- **didn't need to do** = it was not necessary (doesn't matter if he did it or not): He **didn't need to get** up early, so he didn't. *or* ...but he did anyway.
