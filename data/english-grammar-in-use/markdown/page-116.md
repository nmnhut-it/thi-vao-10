# Unit 52 - Question tags (do you? isn't it? etc.)

## A: What are question tags?

**Have you?** and **wasn't it?** are question tags -- mini-questions at the end of a sentence.

We use an auxiliary verb (have/was/will etc.) in question tags. Use **do/does/did** for the present and past simple.

## B: Positive/negative pattern

- **Positive sentence + negative tag:** Kate **will** be here soon, **won't she**?
- **Negative sentence + positive tag:** Kate **won't** be late, **will she**?

| Positive sentence | Negative tag |
|---|---|
| There **was** a lot of traffic, | **wasn't there**? |
| Joe **should** pass the exam, | **shouldn't he**? |

| Negative sentence | Positive tag |
|---|---|
| They **don't** like us, | **do they**? |
| You **haven't** eaten yet, | **have you**? |

**Yes/No** answers to negative sentences:
- 'You're **not** going out this morning, **are you**?' → '**Yes.**' (= I am going out) / '**No.**' (= I am not going out)

## C: Meaning depends on intonation

- **Voice goes down** = not really asking a question, expecting agreement:
  - 'It's a nice day, **isn't it**?' 'Yes, beautiful.'
  - 'Paul doesn't look well today, **does he**?' 'No, he looks very tired.'

- **Voice goes up** = a real question:
  - 'You haven't seen Kate today, **have you**?' 'No, I haven't.'

- **Negative sentence + positive tag** (voice up) = asking for things or asking somebody to do something:
  - 'You couldn't do me a favour, **could you**?'
  - 'You don't know where Karen is, **do you**?'

## D: Special cases

- After **Let's ...**, the question tag is **shall we**: Let's go for a walk, **shall we**?
- After **Don't ...**, the question tag is **will you**: Don't be late, **will you**? *(voice goes down)*
- After **I'm ...**, the negative question tag is **aren't I**: I'm right, **aren't I**?
